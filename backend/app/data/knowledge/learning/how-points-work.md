# How Frequent Flyer Points Work

Frequent flyer points are a type of loyalty currency you earn through everyday activities and redeem for flights, upgrades, and other rewards. In Australia, points can be incredibly valuable — a single point is often worth more than a cent when used wisely, meaning a stash of 100,000 points could get you a business class flight worth thousands of dollars.

## How You Earn Points

There are several ways to build your points balance without ever stepping on a plane:

- **Credit cards:** This is the fastest way most Australians earn points. Many cards offer large sign-up bonuses (sometimes 100,000+ points) and ongoing earn rates of 0.5–1.5 points per dollar spent.
- **Flying:** You earn points when you fly with a program's airline or its partners. The amount depends on your fare class, route, and membership tier.
- **Shopping portals:** Both Qantas and Velocity run online shopping malls where you earn bonus points at hundreds of retailers.
- **Everyday spending:** Fuel, groceries, dining, and insurance can all earn you points through program partners.
- **Transfer partners:** Some credit card reward programs (like American Express Membership Rewards) let you transfer points into multiple airline programs.

## Using Your Points

The most valuable use of points is booking **reward seats** — flights paid for with points instead of cash. Business and first class redemptions typically offer the best value per point. You can also use points for upgrades, gift cards, or merchandise, though these options generally deliver lower value.

## Australia's Main Programs

- **Qantas Frequent Flyer:** Australia's largest program with access to oneworld alliance partners including Cathay Pacific, British Airways, and Japan Airlines.
- **Velocity Frequent Flyer:** Virgin Australia's program, with strong partners like Singapore Airlines, ANA, and United Airlines.
- **KrisFlyer:** Singapore Airlines' program, popular with Australians for premium cabin redemptions across the Star Alliance network.

Each program has its own strengths, and many savvy points collectors participate in more than one to maximise their options.
