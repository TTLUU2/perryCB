# Qantas vs Velocity: Which Program is Better?

Choosing between Qantas Frequent Flyer and Velocity is one of the most common questions for Australian points collectors. The honest answer: both programs have genuine strengths, and the best choice depends on how you travel.

## Earning Ease

**Qantas** has the larger earn ecosystem in Australia. With partnerships spanning Woolworths, BP fuel, and hundreds of retailers, plus a wider range of linked credit cards, it is easier to accumulate Qantas Points through everyday spending. **Velocity** has fewer everyday partners but still offers solid earn rates through credit cards and its own shopping portal. Velocity points are also arguably easier to earn through credit card sign-up bonuses relative to their redemption value.

## Redemption Value

**Velocity** often edges ahead on domestic flights — reward seat pricing for Virgin Australia economy and business class is competitive, and availability tends to be more generous than Qantas. **Qantas** shines on long-haul redemptions through its oneworld partners, especially for premium cabins on Cathay Pacific, Qatar Airways, and Japan Airlines.

## Partner Airlines

**Qantas** gives you access to the oneworld alliance (Cathay Pacific, British Airways, Japan Airlines, Qatar Airways) plus Emirates as a standalone partner. **Velocity** is not in a traditional alliance but has carefully chosen partners including Singapore Airlines, ANA, Etihad, and United Airlines — offering some of the best business and first class products in the world.

## Sweet Spots

- **Qantas:** Sydney to Fiji in business class (36,000 points), partner redemptions on JAL to Tokyo, and Emirates flights to Europe.
- **Velocity:** Singapore Airlines business class from Australia (62,000 points one-way), domestic short-haul in business, and ANA business class to Japan.

## The Verdict

If you fly domestically often and want premium cabin access on Singapore Airlines or ANA, lean towards **Velocity**. If you want the broadest earn ecosystem and access to the oneworld network for international travel, go with **Qantas**. Many experienced collectors participate in both programs to keep their options open.
