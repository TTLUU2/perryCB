# Velocity Frequent Flyer Program Overview

Velocity Frequent Flyer is the loyalty program of Virgin Australia, and it has grown into one of the most compelling points programs in the country. With strong airline partnerships and competitive domestic redemption pricing, Velocity is a favourite among Australian frequent flyers.

## Membership Tiers

Velocity offers four status tiers earned through Status Credits:

- **Red:** Entry level. Access to member fares and points earning.
- **Silver:** 250 SC. Priority check-in, extra baggage, and preferred seating.
- **Gold:** 500 SC. Virgin Australia lounge access, priority boarding, and upgrade eligibility.
- **Platinum:** 1,000 SC. Guest lounge access, highest upgrade priority, and guaranteed seating on sold-out flights (with conditions).

The lower Status Credit thresholds compared to Qantas make Velocity status more achievable for moderate travellers.

## Earning Methods

- **Credit cards:** Numerous Australian credit cards earn Velocity Points directly, with competitive sign-up bonuses often exceeding 100,000 points.
- **Flying:** Earn points on Virgin Australia and partner airline flights.
- **Everyday spending:** Shell fuel, Velocity eStore, and various retail partners.
- **Transfer partners:** American Express Membership Rewards, Citi Rewards, and other bank programs transfer to Velocity.

## Partner Airlines

Velocity is not part of a traditional airline alliance, but has curated a strong set of partners:

- **Singapore Airlines / KrisFlyer:** Book Singapore Airlines business and first class with Velocity Points.
- **ANA (All Nippon Airways):** Access ANA's acclaimed business class product to Japan.
- **Etihad Airways:** Redeem for Etihad flights to the Middle East and Europe.
- **United Airlines:** Reach North America through United's extensive network.
- **Delta Air Lines, Hawaiian Airlines, and South African Airways** round out the partner list.

## Key Redemption Sweet Spots

- **Domestic short-haul business class:** From 15,500 points — excellent value on Virgin Australia's business product.
- **Singapore Airlines business class:** 62,000 points one-way from Australia to Singapore, with good availability.
- **ANA business class to Tokyo:** A standout sweet spot with one of the best business class seats in the sky.
- **Etihad business class to Abu Dhabi:** Competitive pricing with a premium product.

Velocity's reward pricing uses a zone-based chart, and availability on partner airlines tends to be reasonable, particularly on Singapore Airlines routes.
