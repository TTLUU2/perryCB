# Qantas Frequent Flyer Program Overview

Qantas Frequent Flyer (QFF) is Australia's largest loyalty program with over 15 million members. It offers one of the broadest earn-and-burn ecosystems in the country, making it a cornerstone of most Australian points strategies.

## Membership Tiers

QFF has four status tiers based on Status Credits earned through flying:

- **Bronze:** Entry level, earned at 300 SC. Priority check-in and extra baggage.
- **Silver:** 700 SC. Lounge access on Qantas domestic flights and priority boarding.
- **Gold:** 1,400 SC. Oneworld Sapphire status, lounge access across the alliance, and complimentary upgrades.
- **Platinum:** 3,600 SC. Oneworld Emerald, first class lounge access, and the highest upgrade priority.

## Earning Methods

- **Credit cards:** A wide range of cards earn Qantas Points directly, from no-fee options to premium cards offering 1.5+ points per dollar.
- **Flying:** Earn points on Qantas and oneworld partner flights based on fare class and distance.
- **Everyday partners:** Woolworths Rewards, BP fuel, Qantas Wine, Qantas Shopping, and Qantas Insurance all contribute to your balance.
- **Transfer partners:** American Express Membership Rewards and several bank reward programs transfer to QFF.

## Partner Airlines

As a member of the **oneworld alliance**, QFF points can be redeemed on Cathay Pacific, Japan Airlines, British Airways, Qatar Airways, Finnair, Malaysia Airlines, and more. Qantas also has a standalone partnership with Emirates, allowing point redemptions on Emirates flights.

## Key Redemption Sweet Spots

- **Domestic economy:** From 8,000 points + taxes for short-haul routes.
- **Sydney to Fiji business class:** 36,000 points on Fiji Airways — outstanding value.
- **Japan Airlines business class to Tokyo:** 72,000 points one-way from east coast Australia.
- **Emirates business class to Europe:** Competitive rates via the Qantas-Emirates partnership.
- **Cathay Pacific business class to Hong Kong:** A favourite among points enthusiasts for its exceptional hard product.

The Classic Flight Reward table provides fixed-rate pricing, meaning you know exactly what a flight will cost in points regardless of demand — making QFF particularly predictable for planning redemptions.
