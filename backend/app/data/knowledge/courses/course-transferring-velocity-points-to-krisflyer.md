# Transferring Velocity Points to KrisFlyer

**Transferring Velocity Points to KrisFlyer**

A unique feature of the Velocity Frequent Flyer program is the ability to transfer points to Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer. KrisFlyer is an excellent frequent flyer program, so this is a terrific benefit. KrisFlyer miles can be redeemed across the Star Alliance network.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## How it works

Thanks to a unique agreement between Virgin Australia and Singapore Airlines, you can transfer Velocity points into the Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer program - and vice versa. This means there are even more ways to spend Velocity points!

**The transfer rate in both directions is 1.55:1.** For every 1.55 Velocity points transferred, you’ll receive 1 KrisFlyer mile. Likewise, you would need to spend 1.55 KrisFlyer miles to receive 1 Velocity point. So there is effectively a 35% penalty each time you transfer points.

You must transfer at least 5,000 points at a time.

## How to transfer points

To transfer Velocity points to KrisFlyer, you'll need to go to the Velocity Frequent Flyer website.

After logging into Velocity Frequent Flyer, select the “Points Transfer - Airlines” option. To transfer Velocity points to KrisFlyer, you will first need to link your KrisFlyer account to your Velocity account online. This only needs to be done once.

Once your accounts are linked, click “Transfer Now”.

You'll then be able to select the number of points you wish to transfer (with a minimum of 5,000 Velocity points) on the slider.

The points should arrive in your Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer account within a few minutes. (If this does not happen, call the Velocity Membership Contact Centre as something probably went wrong.)

A similar process applies for transfers from KrisFlyer to Velocity. You would need to visit the Singapore Airlines website, go to "Other ways to redeem miles" and scroll down to the "Velocity" section. But there are not many circumstances where it would make sense to transfer KrisFlyer miles to Velocity!

## When to transfer Velocity points to KrisFlyer

There are five main reasons that you might want to transfer Velocity points to KrisFlyer:

## 1. Access to Star Alliance awards

KrisFlyer miles can be redeemed for award seats on all Star Alliance airlines. Of these, most are not Velocity Frequent Flyer partners! So if you're having trouble finding award availability on Velocity partner airlines, this opens a few more options.

If you can't find reward availability on a Velocity partner airline, KrisFlyer miles give you access to awards across the Star Alliance network. Photo: Adobe Stock.

## 2. Access to other partner airline awards

You can also redeem KrisFlyer miles for flights on Vistara, Alaska Airlines and Juneyao Airlines.

You can redeem KrisFlyer miles to fly Alaska Airlines. Photo: Alaska Airlines.

## 3. Access to First Class & Advantage Awards on Singapore Airlines

You **do not necessarily need to convert your Velocity points into KrisFlyer miles if you just want to book a Singapore Airlines flight**! Many Reward Seats are available to book on Singapore Airlines, using Velocity points, directly on the Virgin Australia website.

However, when booking with Velocity points, you'll only have access to a subset of Singapore Airlines flights that have Saver Award availability. Singapore Airlines makes more award seats available as Advantage Awards to KrisFlyer members, which you can only access by redeeming KrisFlyer miles.

Velocity members also do not get access to Singapore Airlines First/Suites awards, which you *can* book using KrisFlyer miles (subject to availability).

Adjoining Singapore Airlines A380 First Class Suites. Photo: Singapore Airlines.

When booking with KrisFlyer miles, you'll also get earlier access to Singapore Airlines Economy, Premium Economy and Business Class award seats. That's because KrisFlyer members can book up to 355 days in advance, while Velocity members only get access to seats 330 days ahead.

## 4. Some KrisFlyer redemptions are better value than Velocity

Velocity and KrisFlyer use completely different award charts, and there are different "sweet spots" in both programs.

For example, Business and First Class flights between Europe and the Middle East may be cheaper if booked as a KrisFlyer award on Star Alliance airlines, compared to redeeming Velocity points.

Turkish Airlines is a Star Alliance member. Photo: Jeffry Surianto from Pexels.

## 5. Upgrading on Singapore Airlines or Star Alliance flights

You can use KrisFlyer miles to upgrade Singapore Airlines flights (provided the booking is in an eligible fare class). You can also use KrisFlyer miles to upgrade some flights on other Star Alliance airlines. These are not things that you can do with Velocity points.

Singapore Airlines Business Class on the regional Airbus A350-900 configuration. Photo: Matt Graham.

## Some things to be aware of

Beware that **KrisFlyer miles expire after 3 years**. So, you may prefer just to transfer the exact amount of Velocity points needed into your KrisFlyer account, as you need them.

Also, it is **not possible to transfer KrisFlyer miles between accounts**. But it is possible to transfer Velocity points to family members, so make sure you complete any necessary family transfers *before* sending your points to KrisFlyer.
