# Marriott Bonvoy

**Marriott Bonvoy**

Marriott Bonvoy sells points that can be transferred to over 40 different airline loyalty programs! This makes it a useful program for buying small quantities of points to transfer to airlines that don't otherwise sell them.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Why buy Marriott Bonvoy points?

Marriott Bonvoy is a hotel loyalty program. But it is still of interest to us because it allows us to (indirectly) buy small quantities of miles for airlines that don't otherwise sell them!

Although Marriott Bonvoy is a hotel loyalty program, you can transfer points from Marriott into many different airline programs. Photo: Jonathan Kemper on Unsplash.

If you need to top-up your KrisFlyer miles, Korean Air Skypass miles, Cathay Asia Miles - or almost any other airline account - Marriott Bonvoy makes this possible.

This unit looks at buying Marriott Bonvoy points for the purpose of transferring them to an airline frequent flyer program. You can read more about Marriott Bonvoy as a hotel loyalty program in Course 9 (Hotel, Supermarket & Car Hire Loyalty Programs).

With Marriott Bonvoy, get points with more niche airlines including Korean Air. Photo: Simon from Pixabay.

## Buying Marriott Bonvoy points

Marriott Bonvoy points cost USD12.50 per thousand. You can purchase points on the Marriott Bonvoy website.

From time to time, Marriott offers discounts of up to 30% (or up to a 60% bonus) on points. These promotions typically occur a few times per year.

There is an annual purchase limit of 100,000 Marriott points. That's the equivalent of only 33,333 airline miles in most programs (excluding bonus miles), meaning this program is most useful for purchases in small quantities.

That said, if you have Amex Membership Rewards points, it is also possible to transfer points from Amex into Marriott Bonvoy.

It takes around two days for the points to post to your account.

## Marriott Bonvoy transfer partners

One of the biggest advantages of Marriott points is the flexibility to transfer to around 38 airline frequent flyer programs! (Of course, you can also redeem points for hotel stays.)

**For every 60,000 Marriott Bonvoy points** that you transfer in one go to most airlines, you'll also **receive 25% bonus airline miles**! In other words, if you transfer 60,000 Marriott Bonvoy points to an airline with a 3:1 transfer rate, you'll receive 25,000 airline miles.

There is a higher bonus for transfers to United MileagePlus. This bonus does not apply when transferring Marriott Bonvoy points to Delta, American Airlines, Avianca or Korean Air.

## Summary: Buying Marriott points

- Points cost USD12.50/thousand

- There is an annual purchase limit of 100,000 miles

- Discounts of 25-30% are offered a few times per year

- You can transfer Marriott Bonvoy points to around 40 airline frequent flyer programs, mostly at a 3:1 ratio

- Earn 25% bonus miles for every 60,000 points transferred to an airline, with a few exceptions
