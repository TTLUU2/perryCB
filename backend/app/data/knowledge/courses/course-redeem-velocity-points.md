Redeem your Velocity Points for the highest possible value!

## Types of Velocity Frequent Flyer redemptions

Just like Qantas points, Virgin Australia's Velocity points can be redeemed either on the ground, or in the air for things like flights and upgrades. You can also spend points on in-flight food and beverage purchases.

Velocity points can be redeemed for the following **non-flight redemptions**:

- Merchandise from the Velocity Rewards store

- Gift cards

- Wine

- Shopping with Myer

- Hotel bookings (powered by Hopper)

- Car hire bookings via Rocket Travel

- Luxury Escapes bookings

You can use Velocity points on Luxury Escapes bookings. Photo: Luxury Escapes.

While **flight redemption** types include:

- Reward Seats

- Any Seat Rewards

- UpgradeMe Points upgrades

- Economy X seat selection

As we'll cover in the next unit, you can also convert your Velocity points into Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer miles.

In general, you'll get much more value out of your points by using them for Reward Seats or upgrades. To put this into perspective, **the following graph shows the typical value of one Velocity point depending on how it is spent** (you can click on the chart to view a larger version):

As you can see, non-flight redemptions generally offer poor value. But **upgrades and premium cabin Reward Seats are among the best ways to use your Velocity points**.

If you have an opportunity to redeem your Velocity points in the air, we would generally recommend saving your points for flights or upgrades instead!

## Points + Pay flights

Points + Pay (previously called Any Seat rewards) can be used to book any seat on any Virgin Australia flight.

This works exactly like Qantas Frequent Flyer's Points Plus Pay. Velocity takes the cash fare and converts it to points at a rate of ~0.59 cents per point. You can choose to pay some or all of the fare using points.

## Reward Seats

Velocity Reward Seats on Singapore Airlines are currently only available in Economy, Premium Economy and Business Class (not First/Suites). Reward seats on Tianjin Airlines remain unavailable at this time.

If you want to redeem your Velocity points to book a flight, Reward Seats are usually the best value option. Velocity Reward Seats have fixed prices based on the distance and airline/s flown, although availability is limited.

After clicking on the flight you want, **Look for seats marked as "Reward"**. The remaining options will be Any Seat rewards, which are just regular airfares converted from dollars into points, and are usually more expensive.

Reward Seats are generally the best value, but aren't available on all flights.

To book reward seats on Capital Airlines, Hainan Airlines, Hong Kong Airlines, South African Airways or Virgin Atlantic,  you would need to call the Velocity Membership Contact Centre on **13 18 75** (within Australia), or **+61 2 8667 5924** (from outside Australia). The call centre is open every day from 08:00-22:00 (Sydney time). You would also need to call for multi-city bookings or for routes not shown on the Virgin Australia website.

## Paying taxes with Velocity points

You'll notice when booking Reward Seats that Virgin Australia displays two point values. The more prominent price at the top includes the taxes, which have been converted to points at the Points + Pay rate of ~0.59 cents per point.

When booking on the Virgin Australia website, you can use the slider to adjust the number of points used to cover the taxes:

Use the slider on the Virgin Australia website to adjust the number of points used to cover the taxes (we recommend paying the minimum number of points required).

We recommend redeeming the minimum number of points required, and paying for the entire amount of taxes using a credit card as this is much better value! You're better off saving the extra points for another reward.

## Award routing rules

Velocity Frequent Flyer has virtually copied the Qantas Frequent Flyer award routing rules. Key Velocity award routing rules include:

- One-way awards are allowed and cost half the price of a return booking

- Stopovers are not permitted. If there is a break between flights of more than 24 hours, the booking will be priced as two separate awards

- A one-way award booking may not exceed 15,000 miles travelled

You can combine flights on Virgin Australia and another partner airline onto the same booking. If you're combining a Virgin Australia flight with another on an airline, or combining two different airlines, the pricing will be based on the sum of the individual segments operated by each airline.

## Point upgrades

Business Class upgrades are another great use of points. Velocity points can be used to upgrade any Virgin Australia Economy Choice or Economy Flex fare. Please note that Economy Lite fares and Reward Seats cannot be upgraded.

"UpgradeMe Points" upgrades on Virgin Australia flights can be confirmed instantly if there is Business Reward seat availability on the flight.

You can view the number of points required for an upgrade on the Velocity website.

## Taxes & carrier charges

**Carrier charges are payable** when redeeming Velocity points to fly with **Virgin Australia**,** Qatar Airways** or **Singapore Airlines**. There are no fuel or carrier surcharges when redeeming Velocity points for reward flights on other partner airlines, but you'll still need to pay any third-party taxes.

For Virgin Australia flights, carrier charges range from $11 per domestic flight to $70 for short-haul international Business class flights.

For travel on other partner airlines, the carrier charge depends on the class of travel & distance travelled. See the Velocity Frequent Flyer website for a complete list by airline.
