# Other Cheap Round-the-World Tickets

**Other Cheap Round-the-World Tickets**

The previous unit looked at the flexible alliance-based round-the-world fares. These are great, but are not your only option! Other types of discounted round-the-world tickets can be even better value.

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Special discounted airline RTW fares

Several airlines offer cheap round-the-world fares that are perfect if you would like to visit 3 or 4 continents with just a few stopovers along the way. These basic round-the-world fares are less flexible and come with fewer stops, but they are very well-priced.

You can even access these discounts on bookings originating right here in Australia. There's no need to position to Japan, Sri Lanka or South Africa to start one of these tickets!

## Lufthansa Group round-the-world fare

The Lufthansa Group's round-the-world ticket is one of the most common. With this fare, you'll fly from Australia to Europe with a stop in either Asia, North America or South Africa along the way. You'll then return back to Australia via your choice of any of the same continents. (You technically don't have to actually fly around the world - for example, you could fly via Asia or North America both ways.)

The flights to, from and within Europe must be on Lufthansa, Swiss or Austrian Airlines via their respective hubs in Frankfurt, Munich, Zurich or Vienna.

A Lufthansa Boeing 747 at Frankfurt Airport. Photo: Matt Graham.

But because the Lufthansa Group airlines do not fly to Australia, the sectors between Australia and the intermediate stopover point may be flown on another airline such as United, Thai Airways, Singapore Airlines, American Airlines or Philippine Airlines. You can even use Qantas to fly in and out of Australia, if you want to.

With a Lufthansa/Swiss/Austrian round-the-world ticket, you can add up to one stopover in each continent between Australia and Europe if you purchase a fare in a higher fare class than the cheapest option. For example, if you travelled to Europe via North America and returned via Africa, you'd get one stopover in each of those continents with a "Z" class Business fare.

You get one stopover at any destination in Europe, regardless of your fare class, however you may utilise an open-jaw. This is when you fly into one city and fly out of another. So for example, you could fly into Copenhagen, make your own way around Europe, and then fly out of Lisbon. You can add an additional stopover in Zurich as well, if you want. Here is one of the many possible routings:

An example of a Lufthansa Group round-the-world itinerary, using Swiss for the sectors to/from Europe.

There is a very long list of stopover options in Europe. In Asia, there are several cities to choose from including Singapore, Bangkok, Hong Kong, Tokyo and Seoul. In North America, options include Los Angeles, San Francisco and New York. For South Africa, the only option is Johannesburg.

A SWISS Airbus A330. Photo: Swiss.

These fares are generally the cheapest among the airlines offering these specialised round-the-world tickets. A Lufthansa Group round-the-world fare in Economy can cost as little as:

- From $2,600 in Economy

- From $5,000 in Premium Economy

- From $8,500 in Business

- From $16,000 in First

Note that these prices are indicative only and will vary.

As with all of the special round-the-world fares covered in this unit, this deal can't be booked online. But you will be able to book with most Australian travel agents. If you don't already have a travel agent, RoundAbout Travel is a reputable online travel agency based in Adelaide that specialises in these tickets.

## Finnair round-the-world fare

   If you'd prefer to stick to Oneworld airlines, Finnair has an excellent round-the-world product of its own. A typical itinerary is:

- Australia to Asia with Qantas

- Asia to Europe with Finnair

- Europe to North America with Finnair

- North America back to Australia with Qantas again.

Finnair Business Class. Photo: Finnair.

Cathay Pacific and British Airways can also be used. It may also possible to add a stop in South America between Australia and North America, but this costs a little extra.

Typical Finnair stopover points in Asia including Singapore, Bangkok, Hong Kong, Seoul, Tokyo and Delhi. In North America, typical stopovers include Los Angeles, Dallas and New York.

Here's an example of a Finnair RTW itinerary:

Example of a Finnair RTW itinerary.

Finnair RTW tickets are often a little cheaper than the Swiss and Lufthansa options in Economy, but may not include things like seat selection on the cheaper fares. Finnair is typically more expensive in Premium Economy and Business, and does not offer First Class.

Finnair RTW fares are a great earner of Qantas Frequent Flyer points and status credits!

## Turkish Airlines round-the-world fare

Turkish Airlines too has its own round-the-world fare. With this one, you can fly to almost anywhere in the Europe or Middle East, via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines.

Turkish Airlines Business Class. Photo: Turkish Airlines.

 To get to Istanbul, you can fly first from Australia to either Asia, North America or Johannesburg with a wide choice of airlines, including Air Canada, Thai Airways, Garuda Indonesia and Qantas. You'd then take Turkish Airlines to Istanbul, and onwards to your final destination in Europe/Middle East (if it's not Istanbul).

You're allowed up to 2 free stopovers (and 2 additional at $50 each) in each direction. Many different routings are possible with this fare, but surcharges apply when using certain airlines or routings.

This fare costs around $2,000-3,200 in Economy or $8,000-$12,000 in Business, depending on the routing used. A cheaper option would be as follows, using Qantas to Jakarta and Air Canada from Toronto to Sydney, with Turkish Airlines for the rest:

## Qantas round-the-world fare

Qantas will allow you to book simple round-the-world itineraries using the multi-city booking tool on the Qantas website. Here's an example:

Flight search on the Qantas website's multi-city booking tool.

You could then go through and select the flights you want on each segment and get a quote directly on the Qantas website:

Sample Qantas round-the-world airfare quote in Economy.

As a general rule, the Qantas website will allow you to route from Australia to North America, then Europe, Asia and finally back to Australia. The same is possible in the other direction. You may also be able to fly between Australia and Europe via Johannesburg.

Most of the sectors will need to be on Qantas flights, with the exception of the trans-Atlantic crossing. This can be on British Airways or American Airlines.

Qantas A380 Economy Class. Photo: Qantas.

You can use Qantas-marketed codeshare flights on Emirates (between Australia, Middle East & Europe), and on American Airlines from North America to Australia.

Prices vary depending on your travel dates, but a typical airfares start from around:

- Economy: $3,000

- Premium Economy: $6,000

- Business: $21,000

- First: $27,000

This can be a good deal if you specifically want to fly Qantas. But overall, other airlines offer better value round-the-world airfares.

## Circle fares

Circle fares aren't *quite* round-the-world fares, but they do allow you to visit multiple continents for a reasonable price.

The Circle fare of most interest to Australians is the Circle Pacific fare. This fare allows you to visit three continents other than Australia, while crossing the Pacific Ocean exactly twice. Here is an example of a Circle Pacific routing:

Example of a Circle Pacific routing.

Another variation of this is the Circle Atlantic ticket. This generally routes from Europe to North America, then South America, and finally back to Europe.

Each of the three alliances - Oneworld, Star Alliance and SkyTeam - sell special-price Circle tickets. (For example, there is some detailed information avaiable about the Oneworld Circle Pacific fare on the Oneworld website.) To book one of these fares, you would need to consult a travel agent.

## Air passes

Round-the-world tickets are not the only special fares offered by the alliances. They each also sell single-continent air passes. These allow you to book multiple flights within a single continent, for one fixed base price, based on the total mileage.

Not all of these passes are particularly good value. But you may find some reasonable value buying an Airpass for travel within South America. Japan Airlines also offers a Japan Explorer Pass for foreign visitors to Japan.

Most air passes cannot be booked online, but are available via travel agents.
