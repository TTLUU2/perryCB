# Operational Upgrades

**Operational Upgrades**

Airlines sometimes give out free upgrades on flights that are oversold in a lower cabin. This unit explains the concept of operational upgrades and how to predict when they might be offered.

## What are operational upgrades?

So far in this course we've showed you ways to pay for an upgrade - using either points or money.

Free upgrades do also exist. But they require a lot of luck!

Airlines sometimes sell more tickets in the Economy cabin than the number of available seats. When this occurs, they'll issue what's known as "operational upgrades", or "op-ups" to a few lucky passengers.

Emirates is one airline that regularly oversells its Economy cabin. On flights where Economy demand is high but Business and First Class demand is lower, Emirates will continue to sell Economy tickets even when the cabin is full. They do this as long as there are enough vacant Business and First Class seats available to upgrade the surplus Economy passengers. We've even seen cases where Emirates has upgraded over 40 passengers on a single flight!

Emirates sometimes sells more Economy seats than the number of physical seats available in that cabin. Photo: Matt Graham.

## How operational upgrades are allocated

If operational upgrades are required, **priority will nearly always be given to frequent flyers with top-tier status**.

There are a few exceptions. For example, Emirates now upgrades passengers that paid the highest fares first. But this is the exception, rather than the rule.

You can never rely on receiving an operational upgrade, but there are some ways to predict the likelihood of op-ups being given on your flight.

If you notice that there are no seats left for sale in Economy, but there are still available Business seats, this could be an indication that operational upgrades are likely.

In some cases, passengers with no status may be offered a free upgrade if they are booked on an oversold flight. But this is generally only offered if the passenger agrees to move to a different flight. This practice normally occurs when the entire flight - not just Economy - is overbooked.

If you have elite status and your cabin class is overbooked, then there is a small chance you'll get a free upgrade! But, remember, the only way to guarantee a bump-up is to pay for it - either using points or cash.
