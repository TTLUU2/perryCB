# Earning Points with Other Financial Products

**Earning Points with Other Financial Products**

If you're unable to get a credit card - or don't want one - there are a few other ways to earn points for spending. For example, you might be able to earn points with a debit card or loan.

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Earning points with a debit card

There are many benefits of credit cards over debit cards. These include:

- Earn a lot more frequent flyer points

- Ability to request a credit card chargeback, and other security features

- Credit cards often include extra benefits such as travel insurance

- When a hotel or car rental company takes a pre-authorisation/deposit, you don't temporarily lose access to your own funds

That said, we fully understand that not everyone wants - or is able - to get a credit card. The good news is that there are some alternatives.

Debit cards (which come with most bank accounts) are widely accepted and generally have low or no fees. And as you'll be spending your own money, you don't need to apply for credit.

Most debit cards don't award points, but there are a few exceptions. This unit looks at several of those options.

## Qantas Pay

Qantas Pay (formerly Qantas Travel Money) is a debit card available to Qantas Frequent Flyer members. This is intended as a prepaid travel money card, but you can also use the card within Australia. There are no fees when using Qantas Pay for domestic transactions, and you'll earn a small amount of Qantas points.

With a Qantas Pay card, you'll earn:

- 1 Qantas Point per $4 spent in Australia (that's 0.25 Qantas Points per dollar), or

- 1.5 Qantas Points per $1 spent in foreign currency

If you're mainly using your card within Australia, you can earn Qantas Points at a higher rate with most credit cards. But 0.25 Qantas Points per dollar isn't bad for a fee-free debit card.

Velocity Frequent Flyer also previously offered its own Velocity Global Wallet card, but discontinued that product in 2021.

Since May 2024, this rebranded card is no longer on the back of physical Qantas Frequent Flyer membership cards.

## Bank accounts that award Qantas Points on the balance

There are several savings accounts available in Australia that award Qantas Frequent Flyer points on the account balance. These accounts may offer lower interest rates than other savings accounts, so do the maths to see whether this is a good deal for you.

The **AMP Bank Everyday account** awards 1 Qantas Point per $10 held in the account each month, up to a maximum balance of $5 million. There are no fees, but you also won't earn any (cash) interest.

The **Qudos Bank Qantas Points Saver** bank account is another option. This account has no fees and you'll earn 400 Qantas Points per $1,000 in the account per year (up to $1 million per year). This savings account also offers a variable interest rate.

The **BOQ Specialist Everyday Plus** account also has no fees and awards 1 Qantas Point **or** 1 Velocity Point per $20 in the account each month up to a balance of $100,000. However, this account earns no interest (other than your choice of Qantas or Velocity Points) and is only available to medical, dental or veterinary professionals & qualified accountants.

## Home loans that earn frequent flyer points

If you're in the market for a home loan, you could take the opportunity to find a loan that earns frequent flyer points.

Some banks and credit unions offer ongoing frequent flyer points over the life of the loan. With other offers, you could receive a large "sign-up" bonus up-front when you apply and are approved for a mortgage. The amounts of points on offer is significant.

Here are a few ways to earn points on your home loan...

## Qantas Money Home Loans

Launched in February 2023, Qantas Money offers home loans which come with 100,000 Qantas Points every year for the life of the loan. You can borrow between $300,000 and $3 million with one of these loans.

You can view an Australian Frequent Flyer forum thread about Qantas Money Home Loans here.

## Qudos Bank Qantas Points Home Loan

Qudos Bank also offers a Qantas Points Home Loan. This gives you 150 Qantas Points per year for every $1,000 of your loan's balance. For example, if you had $500,000 outstanding on your loan, you would earn 75,000 Qantas Points per year.

## Are these products a good deal?

Potentially, but you should shop around as you might be able to get a better interest rate elsewhere.

If you can get a more competitive interest rate with another bank, you should consider how much more you'd be spending on a home loan that comes with Qantas Points. You may end up worse-off overall if you're paying lots of extra interest on a loan just because it earns points. But if the value of those points is worth more than the extra cost, then it may still be worth it.

See our unit on points & status credits if you're unsure how to calculate how much a point is worth. But as a general guide, assigning a value of 1-2 cents per frequent flyer point would be reasonable.

Of course, if the interest rates offered by Qantas Money or Qudos Bank are just as good or better than what you can get elsewhere, then these loans could be a great deal.

The trade-off to earning frequent flyer points on your mortgage could be a higher repayment cost.

It's also worth mentioning that many of the major banks offer their premium credit cards to home loan customers with a waived annual fee. That could save you around $400 per year, depending on the card, and could be a very worthwhile benefit.

This goes without saying... but it's not a good idea to take out a home loan just to get bonus points! These offers are aimed at people who need to get a home loan anyway.

## Sign-up bonus points on home loans

From time to time, banks offer sign-up bonus points on home loans. These are usually limited-time offers. For example, CommBank, Westpac and ANZ have run promotional offers in the past.

In 2023, Velocity and Canstar offered bonus points when applying for a home loan through Canstar. Canstar is a comparison website that promotes home loans from different financial institutions. However, this partnership has since ended.
