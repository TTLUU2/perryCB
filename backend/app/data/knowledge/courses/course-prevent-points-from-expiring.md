# Prevent Points from Expiring

**Prevent Points from Expiring**

Frequent flyer points do not have an unlimited life. Millions are left to expire every year, and many airlines don't adequately warn members in advance. Don't let your points expire!

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Know when your frequent flyer points will expire

It can be easy to lose track of your frequent flyer points, especially if you have more than one account. But there are some simple ways to ensure your hard-earned frequent flyer points never expire!

The first step is to ensure you're familiar with the point expiration policy of each frequent flyer program. Every airline has a different policy, which does make things a bit difficult. But most airlines will have one of the following four policies:

Qantas and Virgin Australia use activity-based expiration:

- **Qantas Frequent Flyer points expire if no points are earned or redeemed for more than 18 months**

- **This also applies to points in Qantas Business Rewards accounts, which expire after 18 months of account inactivity**

- **Virgin Australia Velocity points expire after 2 years of account inactivity**

Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer is an example of a program that uses "time stamping". All KrisFlyer miles expire 3 years after they are earned, regardless of your account activity. Miles continuously expire over time if not used. When redeeming miles, the oldest ones are always used first.

## How to keep track of your points

Not all airlines will warn you when your points are due to expire. So it's important to keep track of them yourself. That way, you can act before the points are lost.

If you only have one or two frequent flyer accounts, keeping track of your points should not be an onerous task. But if you're a member of multiple loyalty programs, it could get a bit difficult.

This is where a service such as AwardWallet can help. It provides a convenient overview of all your loyalty program accounts in one place and warns you by email before your points expire.

AwardWallet can alert you when your points are at risk of expiring soon.

## What to do if your points are about to expire

If you're regularly earning and burning points, this third and final step may not be necessary. But what should you do if you discover that you have a pile of points that are about to expire?

## If the points have a fixed expiry...

If your points are in a program where they expire after a fixed period of time, such as Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, you'll almost certainly need to use them or lose them.

If you think you'll be travelling in the next year or so, it might be a good idea to redeem those points for your trip. It doesn't matter if you plan to travel after the miles expire – you just need to make a booking before they expire.

Even if you have no travel plans, you might as well use your points to get *something*. This may be a hotel stay, a gift voucher or something from the airline's range of merchandise. It may not be the best use of points, but at least you'll get some value out of them.

Some programs allow you to extend the life of your miles for a small fee. This can be used as a last resort. For example, KrisFlyer allows you to keep your miles alive for an extra six months for a fee of USD12 or 1,200 miles for every 10,000 miles. You may want to check whether your frequent flyer program offers a similar service.

## If the points expire due to inactivity...

The advice is different if your points can be kept alive by keeping your account active, like with Qantas or Velocity points. In this case, the solution is relatively simple – you just need to earn or redeem at least one new point before they all expire. That could be as easy as making a purchase via the airline's online mall or buying some wine.

In the case of Velocity Frequent Flyer, just buy something at 7-Eleven and swipe your Velocity card. With Qantas, you could buy something at BP or even earn points for free by using the Qantas Wellbeing App or joining HOYTS Rewards.

Earning Qantas points can be as easy as downloading the free Qantas Wellbeing App.

Just make sure that the points are credited to your account before the expiry date. There may be a delay between earning and receiving the points, so don't leave it to the last minute!

Another option is to simply redeem miles for the cheapest thing available. For example, you could redeem American Airlines AAdvantage miles for a cheap magazine subscription. Or, many programs will let you donate a small amount of points to charity. That's a small price to pay if it keeps the rest of your balance alive.

Alternatively, you could buy a minimum quantity of top-up points from the airline.

Beware that family transfers may not count as activity. If you transfer points into an inactive account, they could expire immediately.

## Hotel bookings

If your points are at risk of expiring due to account inactivity, another easy way to earn a few points is by booking a hotel room.

Even if your points are with an obscure overseas frequent flyer program, you might be able to earn some points with that program by booking your hotel using:

- That frequent flyer program's hotel booking portal,

- Agoda PointsMAX, or

- RocketMiles

With Agoda PointsMAX and RocketMiles, you can choose to credit the points earned from any hotel booking to your choice of dozens of different loyalty programs.

If you have multiple accounts with overseas airlines, simply alternate the programs that you credit to whenever you book a hotel. This ensures there is always some activity in each account.

## What to do if your points have already expired

Where possible, you really shouldn't let your points expire! Once they're gone, there's no guarantee you'll be able to get them back. And even if there is a way, it's unlikely to be free.

Some airlines allow you to buy back expired miles. For example, you can "reactivate" expired American Airlines AAdvantage miles for a fee. See the American Airlines website for details.

## Qantas points reinstatement challenge

If you've had Qantas points expire, you should contact the Qantas Frequent Flyer Service Centre to request a "points challenge". This may be offered on a discretionary basis, and is unlikely to be offered if your points expired more than 12 months ago.

If a challenge is offered, you'll generally be given six months to earn at least 2,500 Qantas points from at least 2 different Qantas Frequent Flyer partners. Upon completion of the challenge, 50% of your expired Qantas points balance will be reinstated.

Qantas may let you reinstate some or all of your expired frequent flyer points with a challenge. Photo: Matt Graham.

Velocity Frequent Flyer does not offer an equivalent challenge but may reinstate expired points in limited circumstances on compassionate grounds.

If all else fails, you should contact the airline as soon as possible and make a compelling case as to why they should reinstate your points as a one-off gesture of goodwill.
