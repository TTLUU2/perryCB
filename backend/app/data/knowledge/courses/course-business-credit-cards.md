## The difference between personal, business & corporate cards

If you run a business and pay for a lot of expenses by card, you may consider supplementing any personal credit cards with a business credit card.

Personal cards are intended primarily for individuals. On the other hand, business credit cards are designed for small-to-medium business owners and their employees making business expenses.

Corporate cards are similar to business cards, but are designed for large businesses with many employees.

Business cards often have higher annual fees than equivalent personal cards. They also come with different benefits and may earn frequent flyer points at different rates.

Some typical features of business cards include:

- Ability to issue employee cards with individual spending limits

- Business-friendly features such as expense tracking

- Higher credit limits

- If the card is for a company, the liability for payments would fall on the business and not an individual

- Some business cards offer complimentary travel insurance, which also covers employee cardholders

- More likely to earn points for business-related spend including with the ATO

Most banks permit a small amount of business-related expenditure on personal credit cards, in conjunction with personal spend. But if you're putting through large amounts of business transactions, you probably should consider a business credit card.

In general, you should only use business cards to process business (and not personal) expenses.

If you run a business, you may find it useful to separate personal and business expenses by using personal and business cards. Business cards often also integrate with accounting software, making it easier to track business expenses made by yourself and your employees.

Business and corporate cards work a bit differently to personal credit cards.

## How business credit cards work

As a business owner, you can apply for a business credit card in the name of the business. You can also choose to issue supplementary cards to your employees, and will be liable to pay any expenses incurred by employees. However, you as the business owner will collect all of the points earned on the card.

To open a business credit or charge card in Australia, your business would need to be established and have an active Australian Business Number (ABN). Your business may also need to be registered for GST and/or earn a minimum level of annual revenue.
