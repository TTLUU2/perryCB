# Alliance-Wide Upgrades

**Alliance-Wide Upgrades**

With Star Alliance, you can use points or miles with any Star Alliance airline to upgrade a flight on any other carrier within the alliance. But this option is quite restrictive, and it's not always worth it.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Star Alliance Upgrade Awards

Normally, you can't redeem points for upgrades on partner airlines. Star Alliance upgrade awards are an exception to this.

Provided there is reward seat availability, you can use points or miles with any Star Alliance airline to upgrade on any other airline within that alliance:

Star Alliance member airlines.

But there are some conditions, and the value is questionable.

Here's how it works...

The process of requesting an alliance-wide upgrade.

You'll be able to upgrade using points or miles with any Star Alliance airline to upgrade on any Star Alliance flight, provided the following conditions are met:

- You must book an eligible full-fare Economy ticket (or full-fare Business class if upgrading to First)

- There must be award availability in the cabin you wish to upgrade into (i.e. Business (or First) on your flight

- You need to have enough miles for the upgrade (mileage costs are set by your frequent flyer program).

You'll need to be booked in an eligible fare class to request a Star Alliance Upgrade Award. See this PDF document on the Singapore Airlines website for a list of eligible fare classes.

TAP Air Portugal's A321neo LR Business Class. Photo: Matt Graham.

In theory, this is a great benefit of Star Alliance. But there are some catches:

- Premium cabin award availability is usually limited

- Generally, only "Y" and "B" fare classes can be upgraded using this award. This does vary, so you'll need to check with your frequent flyer program which fare classes are eligible. But either way, these fares are usually very expensive

- Upgrades are not possible on codeshare flights where the operating airline is different to the marketing airline on your ticket

- Upgrading to or from Premium Economy is not currently possible.

## Is this good value?

The full-fare tickets you'll need to buy in order to even be *eligible* to upgrade can be nearly as expensive as a discounted ticket in the higher cabin class.

Plus, the number of frequent flyer points required for the upgrade is typically almost as high as the cost of an outright reward ticket. But the availability is the same. So, quite honestly, these upgrade awards are of limited practical use. But they could be handy, for example, if your company has an Economy-only travel policy.

See the Star Alliance website for more information.

## Alliance-wide upgrades on SkyTeam and Oneworld

## SkyTeam

The SkyTeam alliance no longer has a formal Mileage Upgrade program, but there are a small handful of SkyTeam airlines that will let you redeem miles to upgrade on selected other SkyTeam airlines.

Participating frequent flyer programs include:

- Flying Blue (Air France, KLM, Kenya Airways & TAROM) + see here for list of eligible fare classes

- Korean Air SKYPASS

Korean Air Business Class. Photo: Korean Air.

SkyTeam upgrades are only available on international flights. Like Star Alliance upgrades, only bookings in the highest fare classes are eligible and there must be award availability. (Thankfully, SkyTeam award availability is often pretty good.)

## Oneworld

The Oneworld alliance has announced that it plans to implement its own alliance-wide upgrade system. However, the implementation of this has been repeatedly delayed. Watch this space.

In the meantime, it is possible to upgrade on British Airways, Iberia or American Airlines using Avios or miles with any of these airlines' programs. For example, see the American Airlines website for details on upgrading with AAdvantage miles.

You can upgrade Iberia flights with American Airlines AAdvantage miles, subject to certain conditions. Photo: Iberia.

Also keep in mind that you can transfer Avios between your accounts with British Airways, Iberia, Qatar Airways and (from March 2024) Finnair. For example, if you had British Airways Executive Club Avios and wanted to upgrade a Qatar Airways flight, you could transfer British Airways Avios to Qatar Avios at a 1:1 conversion rate.
