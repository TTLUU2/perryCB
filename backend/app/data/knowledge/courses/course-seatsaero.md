# Seats.aero

**Seats.aero**

Seats.aero is a powerful and user-friendly tool that can help you instantly find reward seats on a wide range of airlines, routes & dates.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## How Seats.aero works

Seats.aero is one of the most useful and powerful new award search tools for both flights and hotels. With this tool, you can see award availability across many different routes and dates on one page - you don't have to search for one route or date at a time!

You can instantly see award availability across the next two months for free. And with a Pro subscription, you can view available seats over the next year.

When searching with Seats.aero, the results are almost instant. You can then easily sort and filter your results to find options that you really want. You can also set up award seat alerts, and instantly view flights to or from any airport that's covered by the site.

Here are some of the useful features offered by Seats.aero...

## Explore mode

In the “Explore” mode, you can search for award availability for any of the routes and frequent flyer programs that Seats.aero supports. This includes Qantas Frequent Flyer and Velocity Frequent Flyer.

You can view flights from any origin to any destination, or filter by region (Oceania, Asia, Europe, etc.). If you're looking for flights to or from a specific airport, you can enter the IATA airport code in the "search" box to only show flights to or from that airport.

The minimum number of points required to book a seat on that route and date will be shown for each cabin class with availability. Results shown in green are direct flights. Blue boxes indicate indirect flights.

Using the Seats.aero “Explore” mode, you can view award availability on Qantas and its partner airlines, filtering by origin, destination, date, cabin class and more.

One or more flights could have award seats available on each date and route shown in the search results. To view more information about available flights, click on the “i” box to the right. This will then bring up a box with more details about the flight times, points and taxes required.

Click on the “i” button to view more details about any of the options found on your Seats.aero search.

By default, the search results are in order by departure date. Direct flights are also shown before indirect options. But you can click on any of the headings to sort the results using another criteria. For example, if you want to show flights with Premium Economy availability at the top, click on "Prem Economy".

You can click on any of the cabin classes to show options in that cabin at the top of the results, such as Premium Economy flights in this example. 

## Search feature

Have points available with more than one frequent flyer program, and want to search for reward availability on a specific route & date? Use the "Search" option to look for flights between specific airports (you can enter more than one origin and destination if you want). This will then look for options across all airlines & frequent flyer programs, not just with one specific program.

You can also conduct a search for a specific route/date that combines results for all of the frequent flyer programs that Seats.aero covers.

## Creating alerts

Seats.aero lets you create reward seat alerts for specific routes and cabin classes with any of the supported programs. You’ll then receive an email or SMS if Seats.aero identifies any award availability that matches your search criteria in the future.

You can also use Seats.aero to set up award flight alerts.

You can also set award alerts with ExpertFlyer, but only for one specific flight and cabin class at a time.

## Other features

Other Seats.aero features include a Lufthansa First Class Finder, ANA First Class Finder, United PlusPoints Finder and a Qatar Qsuites Finder.

Qatar Airways Business Class Qsuites. Photo: Qatar Airways.

Seats.aero also has a Great Circle map tool, a fare class viewer (much like Expert Flyer) and a visa-check feature via IATA Timatic.

## Limitations

While Seats.aero does a very good job, there are a few known limitations:

- Results are cached, so may be out of date. However, you can easily see when the availability was last checked, and if you click on any flight option the result will refresh.

- Not all routes are covered, although Pro members can request for new routes to be added.

- When searching with Explore mode, Seats.aero doesn't show seats that don't appear on your airline's website (e.g. South African Airways reward seats don't appear when searching for Velocity Frequent Flyer availability).

## Subscription options

You can view award availability for travel in the next 60 days for free, without creating an account. You can also create email alerts without a paid account.

If you choose to purchase a Pro account for USD9.99/month or USD99.99/year, you can get access to more features including:

- Search for award availability up to a year in advance

- Access advanced filters including direct flights and minimum number of seats (this works with most but not all programs)

- The option to receive SMS notifications, rather than just emails

- Use the United PN/PZ Finder (United’s special fare classes for upgrade availability)

- Access the Seats.aero Discord server
