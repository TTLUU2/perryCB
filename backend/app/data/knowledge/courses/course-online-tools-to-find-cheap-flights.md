## ITA Matrix

ITA Matrix is arguably *the *most powerful and useful online tool for finding cheap flights! It can handle all types of bookings – from simple one-way flights, right through to complicated multi-city itineraries. And it offers a degree of flexibility in your searches that is really second-to-none.

Easily compare prices, flight times and connections by viewing ITA Matrix results as Time Bars. You can also search for flights to/from multiple airports in one search.

You can view prices over a whole month, and even search for flights from multiple origins and to multiple destinations. This makes it very easy to find the cheapest flights for your specific needs in just one search!

Where ITA Matrix really excels is the "advanced controls" feature. These can be used to really narrow down any search, or to search for a very specific combination of flights.

You can't actually book through ITA Matrix, but you'll be provided with a full fare breakdown and a link to the ticketing rules.

ITA Matrix has so much useful functionality, in fact, that we've dedicated an entire unit to it. The next unit contains a detailed overview of ITA Matrix's features, including a full list of advanced controls, and a video demonstration of how to use it.

## Key features

- Handles one-way, return and multi-city flight searches

- Search for flights from multiple origins and/or to multiple destinations simultaneously

- Filter the results with powerful "advanced controls"

- Search up to one month at a time

- See the flight time, aircraft type and fare class of all options on one screen

- Great for researching status runs

## Google Flights

Google Flights' Explore mode.

Google Flights, the successor to ITA Matrix, has emerged as one of the best websites and most user-friendly websites for finding cheap flights.

The powerful search engine is simple to use. As well as comparing prices, you'll even be able to see details like the amount of legroom, in-flight entertainment, and whether or not wifi is offered on board.

Once you've selected your flights, you'll be redirected to either the airline's, or a third-party website to complete the booking.

If you're after some inspiration, you can always leave the destination field blank. Google Flights will then show you a range of possible destinations and prices on a map. **This can be a handy feature for discovering great airfare deals, including error fares!**

Unfortunately, the prices quoted by Google Flights are not always 100% reliable. And it doesn't handle complex multi-city bookings well.

## Key features

- Easy find the cheapest destination/s for your travel dates

- Detailed flight information including aircraft configurations, availability of wifi, legroom etc.

- User-friendly

AFF has a helpful guide to Google Flights with more details about its features.

## Skyscanner

The Skyscanner website.

Skyscanner is a popular website for finding cheap one-way and return flights.

It's not a booking site as such, rather an *aggregator *like Trivago is for hotels. It searches on dozens of airline and third-party websites to find both the lowest priced flights, as well as the cheapest places to *book *those flights. This is one of the reasons it's so popular.

Skyscanner allows fairly flexible searches. You can easily compare prices over a whole month, or even year! If you just want to get on a plane for as little as possible, you can even search for the cheapest flights to anywhere by typing the word "everywhere" into the destination field.

Once you've found a flight you're happy with, Skyscanner will transfer you to the listed third-party website to complete the booking. But beware that some online travel agents, including those linked from Skyscanner, are not reputable.

Skyscanner is easy to use but it does have some limitations. It's only really useful for simple one-way or return bookings. Multi-city searches are possible, but the engine does not handle complex itineraries well. There are also a small number of airlines that don't show up in the search results.

## Key features

- Compares booking prices on different airline and online travel agent websites

- Search for the cheapest flights across an entire month (or year)

There are many other flight search websites that are also more than adequate for finding cheap one-way and return flights. **Kayak**, **Momondo**, **Expedia** and **Priceline** are a few of the most popular ones. Feel free to check out all of them and choose your favourite.

## AZair

AZair website.

AZair offers a unique service, finding the cheapest fares on low-cost carriers. You've probably never heard of AZair, but it is the best resource for finding the absolute cheapest flights around Europe, the Middle East and Asia. It is most useful for low-cost carrier flight searches for travel in Europe.

The real beauty of AZair is that it will find the absolute cheapest flight combinations, even if they involve random stopovers or multiple tickets. (Few other search engines will consider combining separate tickets!) The search engine is very powerful, so it's a great resource if you *really *want to save money on low-cost carrier flights.

The main downside of using AZair is that full service airlines are generally not listed. There is a full list of airlines covered on the AZair website.

Nowadays, Google Flights may show some of these more creative flight combinations as well.

## Key features

- Find cheap flights on low-cost carriers

- Will find options combining separate tickets if this works out cheaper

- Fares are validated in real-time

## Indie Boots-n-All

Search for multi-stop flights with Indie.

Indie Boots-n-All is best-suited to anyone looking to save money on multi-city and simple round-the-world bookings. After inputting the cities you want to visit, and the dates you want to travel, the website will come up with the cheapest combination of flights using a mixture of full-service and budget airlines.

The tool is very powerful, telling you if a cheaper flight is available within 3 days of your preferred travel dates. It will also look for flights to and from nearby airports to bring down costs.

This could be an alternative to using the multi-city flight search feature on Google Flights.

## Wikipedia airport check

The resources we've covered in this unit are reasonably comprehensive. Unfortunately, no single tool can find *every* available flight. Sometimes, certain airlines are omitted from the search results. And sometimes the search engine overlooks left-field connections. This can make it difficult to be 100% sure you're getting the absolute best possible price.

Sometimes it's a good idea to double-check that you've been presented with *all *available options. If you're flying to an airport you're unfamiliar with, it's always a good idea to check the Wikipedia page for that airport. Every airport's Wikipedia page contains an up-to-date list of all the airlines serving that airport and the destinations they fly to.

This list can be found under the "Airlines and destinations" heading:

If you do discover that an airline or flight hasn't appeared in your search, you can then go directly to that airline's website and check prices.

## Google flight schedules

You probably already know that you can Google the weather at your destination. Google can also be used to quickly calculate exchange rates, for example, by searching “AUD to USD”.

You can also use Google easily check which airlines serve a particular route, and what their schedules are.

Simply type the origin and destination airport codes into Google, and add the word "schedule" or "flight schedule". For example, searching for "SYD SIN flight schedule" will bring up a list of airlines and their current schedules from Sydney to Singapore:

## FlightConnections

If you prefer to look at things visually, FlightConnections.com shows all flight routes worldwide on one map.

With the free version, you can search for all current operating flights to/from any airport, on any route or with any airline. If you upgrade to the premium version, you can also filter by aircraft type, classes of travel, etc. This can be a very useful visual tool for checking which airlines fly where.

FlightConnections.com screenshot

If you want to search for domestic routes with widebody aircraft, there's also a useful free website called Where The Widebodies Are.
