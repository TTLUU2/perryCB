# Round-the-World using Points

**Round-the-World using Points**

Booking a round-the-world trip can be an excellent use of frequent flyer points! This unit explains how to redeem points with Qantas, Cathay Pacific and Singapore Airlines to book a round-the-world trip.

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Qantas Oneworld Classic Flight Reward (Oneworld Award)

Instead of paying for a round-the-world ticket, you can redeem your Qantas points for a "Oneworld Classic Flight Reward". This is also sometimes known as the "Qantas Oneworld Award".

This is a special type of award that can be used to travel around the world – although this is **not** an actual requirement.

If you're planning a round-the-world trip or a complex multi-city booking, this is by far the **best value use of Qantas points**!

Just about any combination of flights is possible with a Oneworld Classic Flight Reward, provided the following conditions are met:

- Up to 5 stopovers allowed

- Can travel up to 35,000 miles in total

- Up to 16 flight sectors allowed in total

- "Surface segments" are permitted, and count as one stopover (there is no official limit, but in practice you'll often be restricted to 2 surface segments)

- Must travel on at least 2 Oneworld airlines other than Qantas

- Up to 1 stopover and 2 transits per city

- Travel may only be completed on Oneworld airlines (Emirates, Jetstar, China Eastern etc. are not Oneworld airlines and cannot be used)

By definition, stopovers are **greater than** 24 hours. Unlimited "transfers" of **under **24 hours are permitted. This can be a good loophole if you would like to spend an extra day/night somewhere but have used up your stopover allowance.

## How to book a Oneworld Classic Flight Reward with Qantas points

You can generally book Oneworld Classic Flight Rewards on the Qantas website, using the multi-city booking tool. **Do not use the "round-the-world" booking tool** as this is only for paid Oneworld Explorer airfares!

If you can't book online, you'll need to call Qantas. However, we recommend trying to book online if you can.

If you require assistance with planning or booking a Oneworld Classic Flight Reward, you may wish to request a quote from our Frequent Flyer Concierge team.

**If booking on the Qantas website, there are various limitations to be aware of:**

- You cannot search for more than 6 flight segments

- Your itinerary cannot include more than 2 surface sectors (airport changes in the same city are fine)

- The first flight cannot commence in a country that the Qantas website does not support (e.g. Qatar, Sri Lanka, Morocco or Peru)

- The itinerary cannot include airports for which the Qantas website does not support Classic Flight Reward bookings, such as some airports in Laos, Botswana, Kazakhstan or Mozambique

- Your itinerary cannot include British Airways operated domestic flights within the UK in Business Class (BA domestic flights operated by a subsidiary like BA Cityflyer are fine)

- If there are more than 10 flight sectors in your itinerary, the Qantas website will not be able to price it.

## Cathay Oneworld Multi-Carrier Award

Cathay Pacific's Oneworld multi-carrier award is very similar to the Qantas Oneworld Classic Flight Reward. It's cheaper, but you also have a lower limit on the number of flight sectors.

This award entitles you to multiple stopovers, and can even be used to travel around the world – although this is also **not** a requirement.

Cathay's frequent flyer program offers a Oneworld multi-carrier award. Photo: Matt Graham.

Just about any combination of flights is possible with a Cathay Oneworld Multi-Carrier Award, provided the following conditions are met:

- Up to 5 stopovers (of any length) allowed

- Up to 2 additional transfers (under 24 hours) allowed

- Up to 8 flights allowed in total

- Up to 2 "surface segments" are also permitted

- Any given airport may be passed through a maximum of twice

- The itinerary may only include Oneworld airlines

- Must travel on either

Two Oneworld airlines other than Cathay Pacific, or

- 3 or more Oneworld airlines where one is Cathay Pacific

For the purposes of this award, Cathay considers a stopover to be a break between flights of any length (more or less than 24 hours). Up to 2 additional "transfers" of under 24 hours are also permitted, for a total of up to 8 flights.

Oneworld Multi-Carrier Awards are priced based on the highest class of travel used and total distance flown. The distance flown includes any surface sectors. If you finish in a different location to the award's origin, the distance between the end point and the origin also counts towards the total distance.

Award prices are available on the Cathay website. Note that additional taxes & carrier charges are also payable.

Cathay's Oneworld multi-carrier award chart as of 1 October 2023.

For the sake of comparison, a Business class round-the-world trip of up to 35,000 miles distance travelled would cost 265,000 Asia Miles. A similar booking made as a Qantas Oneworld Classic Flight Reward costs 318,000 Qantas points.

If your itinerary includes four or less cities and no sectors on American Airlines or Iberia, you may be able to book on the Cathay Pacific website.

## Round-the-world awards with other airlines

The Qantas Frequent Flyer and Cathay redemptions are the best value and most useful round-the-world awards for Australians. But there are several other international frequent flyer programs with special round-the-world award tickets. These include:

- British Airways Executive Club (see this AFF article for more information)

- Lufthansa Miles&More

- Thai Airways Royal Orchid Plus

- Asiana Club

- ANA Mileage Club

- Japan Airlines Mileage Bank

- Iberia Plus (see the "Using Avios" conditions on this page)
