# Find Qantas &amp; Oneworld Reward Availability

**Find Qantas & Oneworld Reward Availability**

This unit demonstrates how to find reward availability for Qantas & other Oneworld airline flights.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Where to find Oneworld reward availability

There are four particularly useful airline websites that show reward availability for all Oneworld airlines:

- Qantas website

- British Airways website

- Japan Airlines website

- American Airlines website

In this unit, we'll explain why each website is useful and how to search for reward availability on each one.

## Qantas website

The Qantas website shows award availability across all Oneworld airlines, although Qatar Airways only releases seats to Qantas a maximum of 30 days prior to departure.

You can even view a month-long calendar of availability **by using the multi-city booking tool**, which makes things easier on hard-to-find routes. (You don't necessarily have to search for more than one flight when using the multi-city booking tool.)

Use the multi-city search tool to view a calendar of Qantas Classic Reward availability.

If you're using Qantas points, you can then click through to book directly on the Qantas website. Otherwise, contact the frequent flyer program that you're using to book the flight.

Unfortunately, the Qantas website does have a few glitches:

- It does not automatically search for all possible routings, so may miss options that require multiple stops

- Sometimes the website says there is premium cabin availability, but the longest flight is actually in Economy

- The opposite can also happen - the website displays no availability in the premium cabin, even though the longest flight is in Business

- The website sometimes shows "phantom" award availability that doesn't actually exist and cannot be booked

- You can't search for flights originating in many countries, including Qatar, Sri Lanka, Oman, Morocco and most of South America & Africa

- The website may show available flights operated by Qantas partners that aren't part of Oneworld, which can make it difficult if you're specifically looking for Oneworld availability

A common sight on the Qantas website: Your trip from Adelaide to Los Angeles will be in Business Class... except for the long flight from Sydney to Los Angeles!

You need to be a Qantas Frequent Flyer member to search for award availability on the Qantas website. If you're reading this, hopefully you're already a member! But if you're not, our training unit on earning Qantas points explains how to join for free.

## Qantas Classic Plus availability

In April 2024, Qantas introduced Classic Plus Flight Rewards. These are not traditional reward seats, but commercial airfares converted into points with dynamic pricing used.

Note that any Classic Plus reward seats shown are *only* available to book through Qantas Frequent Flyer, and not through other Oneworld programs.

When searching on the Qantas website, you'll be able to see both Classic and Classic Plus reward seats in the calendar.

You can't find Classic Plus seats using the multi-city calendar.

## British Airways website

The British Airways website also shows award availability across all Oneworld member airlines. It does a relatively good job, and helpfully shows you how many seats are available on each flight. However, there is a glitch where QantasLink flights may not be shown.

The British Airways website doesn't show reward availability for QantasLink flights. Photo: Matt Graham.

The main downside of the British Airways website is that you'll only be able to search for seats one day at a time. With Qantas, you can display availability across a whole month in one search.

You will have to be a member of the British Airways Club program to search on their website. Registration is free and you'll be given an account number instantly. Once you have this account number, you'll be able to start searching straight away.

## Japan Airlines website

The Japan Airlines website shows award availability (including the number of available seats) on all Oneworld airlines when conducting a "oneworld Award Tickets" search. It also tends to be more reliable and accurate than other websites, and helpfully shows the number of award seats available.

You can search for Oneworld award availability on the Japan Airlines website.

The Oneworld Award searching tool does a marginally better job that the Qantas and British Airways websites at finding creative routings on Oneworld airlines. But it's not the most user-friendly website and you can only search one day & class of travel at a time.

The Japan Airlines website is also useful for confirming that award seats you've found on other airline websites are not "phantom" award availability. It's good for double-checking.

## American Airlines website

The American Airlines website is a final option for finding Oneworld airline award availability. This website also shows award availability across all Oneworld airlines, and you don't need to be an American Airlines AAdvantage member to search for award flights. Simply click "Redeem Miles" when searching for flights.

The American Airlines website has a useful "calendar" feature that allows you to view award availability over an entire month at a time. You can even sort the itineraries displaying in the calendar by the class of travel and number of stops.

Use the American Airlines website's calendar feature to search for availability a month at a time.

When using the American Airlines to search for award availability, beware that most of the American Airlines flights shown are not available to book using other frequent flyer programs. American Airlines also has some partners such as Etihad that aren't bookable with Qantas points, for example.

## Where to find Classic Reward availability on other Qantas partner airlines

Want to use Qantas points to fly a non-Oneworld airline? Classic Reward availability for non-Oneworld partner airlines is also conveniently displayed on the Qantas website. This includes Emirates, Fiji Airways, China Eastern, Jetstar and LATAM Airlines availability.
