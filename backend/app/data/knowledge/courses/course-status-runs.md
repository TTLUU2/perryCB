# Status Runs

**Status Runs**

Just short of the next status tier? A status run could bridge the gap.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## What is a status run?

If you're a frequent flyer, you may be able to reach top-tier airline status easily without going out of your way. But if you happen to be a little short on status credits, a status run could be the answer. (These are also sometimes known as "mileage runs".)

**A status run is when you book a flight/s for the main purpose of earning status credits.** If you're only a little short of the required status credits, this could be the most practical way to reach the higher tier. You'll probably spend a few hundred dollars on flights, but in return you'll receive the benefits of the higher status level for at least a year.

## Maximum status credits for minimum price

The key to status runs is to **find a route that earns the maximum number of status credits, for the lowest possible price**. This usually means flying an indirect routing and taking advantage of the sweet spots within your program's earning table.

## Comfort seats

Qantas award more status credits for more expensive flexible tickets. So buying flexible tickets is one way to double your status credits without flying twice as much. But **booking a comfort seat is another clever hack you can use to earn double status credits when you fly**.

A comfort seat is a guaranteed empty neighbouring seat on the plane. These are usually sold to larger passengers that require the extra space.

But that's not all. Comfort seats have a couple of other little-known benefits:

- **The extra seat earns points and status credits**. (That's in addition to the points and status credits you'll earn for your own seat.)

- **You don't have to pay taxes** for the comfort seat.

Here's an example where it makes perfect sense to book a comfort seat. This Qantas flight from Sydney to Ballina costs $99 for a Red e-Deal fare:

Qantas airfares from SYD to BNK.

By clicking on "Flight amount breakdown" when booking on the Qantas website, we can see that taxes make up $29.14 of the fare:

SYD-BNK fare breakdown on the Qantas website.

The extra cost for a comfort seat is only the base fare of $69.86. That brings the total cost for the two seats to $168.86, and you would earn 20 status credits.

Buying an Economy Flex ticket would also earn you 20 status credits… but at more than double the cost! Plus, you'll have a guaranteed free seat next to you with a comfort seat.

There are **a few things to note about comfort seat bookings**:

- Comfort seats can only be booked over the phone

- You have to purchase the comfort seat with your original ticket (i.e. you can't just
"add" it later)

- You won't be able to select your seat online

- If upgrading, you would need to pay for two upgrades as the comfort seat carries through to the higher cabin

- The points and status credits for the second seat might not credit automatically to your account - call the airline if this happens

## Use Family Pooling

If you're trying to earn status with Virgin Australia, don't forget that the Velocity Frequent Flyer program offers Family Pooling!

This is one of the biggest shortcuts available to Virgin Australia status. By linking your account with other family members, you can pool the entire family's status credits into one account. If you have six members in your family, that could mean earning status six times as fast!
