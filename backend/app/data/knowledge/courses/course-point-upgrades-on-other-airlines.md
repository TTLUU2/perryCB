# Point Upgrades on Other Airlines

**Point Upgrades on Other Airlines**

So far, we've shown you how to upgrade using points on Qantas and Virgin Australia. Many other airlines have similar processes for upgrading with frequent flyer points.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Tips for upgrading with points

Each frequent flyer program has their own policies regarding upgrades using points or miles. As well as different pricing, they may have different rules about:

- Which fare types are eligible for upgrades

- Whether "two-step" upgrades are allowed (e.g. upgrading from Economy to Business Class where Premium Economy is available) or only "one class" upgrades

- Whether the upgrade can be confirmed instantly

- Whether you can upgrade on partner airlines

## General principles for upgrading with points

Here are some common rules to keep in mind...

- You can generally only upgrade **on your frequent flyer program's own airline** (i.e. not partner airlines). For example, you can use Qantas points to upgrade on Qantas flights but not on Emirates flights. You would need Emirates Skywards miles to upgrade an Emirates flight.

- You must **book a fare class that is eligible for upgrading with points**. Most airlines do not allow point upgrades from discounted sale fares. Some airlines take this a step further, and only allow upgrades from the most expensive flexible fares.

- Upgrades **may only be possible to the next available class of travel**. Airlines don't generally allow upgrades directly from Economy Class into First Class (where Premium Economy and/or Business Class is also available).

- Upgrades **from more expensive fare types generally require fewer miles**.

- Most airlines will **confirm your upgrade immediately if there is award availability in the higher cabin**. This happens when award & upgrade space availability is identical. If this is the case, check for award/upgrade seat availability using the airline's website or Expert Flyer before booking - if there is award availability, you can also upgrade!

If Premium Economy is offered on your flight, booking Premium Economy is often a good strategy if you want to upgrade to Business. Anecdotally, you may be more likely to get upgraded. You'll also pay less for the upgrade. And in the case you're unsuccessful, at least you won't have to fly in Economy. ;)

## Check if your fare class is eligible to be upgraded

Many airlines will tell you at the time of booking whether your fare is eligible for an upgrade. For example, here's a screenshot from the Singapore Airlines website, with the conditions for each fare family clearly displayed:

If you wanted to upgrade with KrisFlyer miles on this Singapore Airlines flight, it would be advisable to check for Business Saver award availability before booking. That's because award & upgrade availability is identical on Singapore Airlines. If there is a Business award seat, you'll also be able to upgrade.

However, you should also compare the cost (in terms of money & miles) of buying an Economy ticket & upgrading, versus the cost of booking a Business award seat. The number of miles needed to book an outright award ticket may only be slightly higher than the miles required for an upgrade - and you'll save a lot of cash!

## Specific frequent flyer program upgrade policies

Rules and pricing varies between frequent flyer programs, so check your airline's website if you're unsure.

As a few examples, here are the upgrade policies of several key overseas frequent flyer programs of interest to Australians:

## Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer

Singapore Airlines regional Business Class. Image: Singapore Airlines.

KrisFlyer miles can be used to **upgrade on Singapore Airlines flights**, but **only from Standard & Flexi fare types**. Upgrades from Economy to Business are allowed even if Premium Economy is offered.

See the Singapore Airlines website for more information, where you can also find a chart (PDF) showing the number of KrisFlyer miles needed.

You can also **redeem KrisFlyer miles to upgrade on other Star Alliance airlines** - but only if you've bought an expensive ticket in an eligible fare class, and there is award availability. We'll explain how this works in the next unit on alliance-wide upgrades.

## Emirates Skywards

Emirates has an on-board bar for Business & First passengers on the A380. Photo: Emirates.

You can spend Emirates Skywards miles for **one-class upgrades on Emirates flights** only. There is an exception for upgrades from Economy to Business where Premium Economy is sold on the aircraft.

Emirates allows upgrades from all fare types except Emirates Special & Classic Reward tickets. However, the number of miles required is lower if you've purchased a more expensive ticket type. See the Emirates Miles Calculator to check the number of miles required.

Award & upgrade availability is the same. You can upgrade using Skywards miles any time before your flight if there is Saver Classic Reward availability - or even on board, if there are still empty seats available.

If you have Emirates Skywards miles and want to fly Business Class, upgrading could actually be a good strategy to avoid Emirates' high carrier charges on Classic Reward bookings.

## Etihad Guest

Etihad Airways A350 Business Class. Photo: Etihad.

Etihad Guest miles can only be used to **upgrade Etihad Airways flights**. Most fare types are eligible to be upgraded (except award tickets), but the number of miles is lower if you've bought a more expensive fare type.

You can check the number of miles needed to upgrade using Etihad's Miles Calculator.

## Qatar Airways Privilege Club

Qatar Airways Boeing 787-9 Business Class. Photo: Qatar Airways.

You can redeem Qatar Airways Avios to **upgrade to a higher cabin on any Qatar Airways flight**, subject to award availability. You can upgrade from Economy to Business, Business to First, or Economy to First on flights where there is no Business Class cabin (i.e. short flights within the Middle East). All fare types can be upgraded except Economy Promo.

You **won't receive lounge access or a higher baggage allowance when upgrading** on Qatar Airways, and Avios upgrades to A380 First Class aren't available on the Doha-London route. See the Qatar Airways website for more information.

## American Airlines AAdvantage

American Airlines Boeing 777 Business Class. Photo: American Airlines.

All American Airlines flights can be upgraded using AAdvantage miles, except Basic Economy and award tickets, subject to availability. Your upgrade can be applied to up to 3 American Airlines flight sectors on the same one-way itinerary (e.g. if you're flying Sydney-Los Angeles-Miami, you can upgrade on both sectors and pay for just one upgrade).

On "discount" tickets (which includes all Economy fare classes other than full "Y" class), a cash co-payment is required in addition to the AAdvantage miles. See the American Airlines website for more information.
