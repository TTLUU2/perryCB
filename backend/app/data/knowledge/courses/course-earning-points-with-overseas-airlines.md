# Earning Points with Overseas Airlines

**Earning Points with Overseas Airlines**

Overseas frequent flyer programs can open the door to more redemption opportunities and better value. This unit looks at ways to collect points with international airlines from Australia.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Why overseas frequent flyer programs can be valuable

As we've just explored, there are many ways to earn Qantas points and Velocity points in Australia.

Those frequent flyer programs are valuable. But there are many things you can do with overseas frequent flyer programs that you simply can't do using Qantas points or Velocity points.

Many international frequent flyer programs also offer better value, as they charge fewer points and/or lower carrier surcharges on some types of reward bookings. They can also give you access to reward seats on different partner airlines that you can't access through Qantas Frequent Flyer or Velocity. So, it can be worth diversifying your points portfolio!

Here are just a few examples of international programs that can be worth using...

## Many Australians value KrisFlyer miles

KrisFlyer is the loyalty program of Singapore Airlines. With over a million Australian members, KrisFlyer is the most popular frequent flyer program in Australia that belongs to an airline not based here.

Singapore Airlines is part of Star Alliance, along with Air New Zealand and many other carriers. Photo: Matt Graham.

Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer is popular with Australians for numerous reasons. Here are some of the main ones:

- Singapore Airlines generally has good award seat availability to/from Australia in Premium Economy, Business and (sometimes) First Class

- You can redeem KrisFlyer miles to fly across the entire Star Alliance network

- There are no fuel or carrier surcharges when redeeming KrisFlyer miles for Singapore Airlines flights

- The number of KrisFlyer miles required for a reward flight is generally lower than the equivalent Qantas or Velocity points needed for a similar redemption

- It's somewhat easy to earn KrisFlyer miles in Australia with credit cards

- You can transfer points between the Virgin Australia Velocity and KrisFlyer programs (this is an unusual perk)

As an example of the value of KrisFlyer, you can often find multiple Business Class award seats from Australia to Asia and Europe when booking well in advance. Here's one example of Singapore Airlines Business Class award availability from Perth to London:

KrisFlyer is one of few frequent flyer programs where you can often find Business Class award availability from Australia to Europe for a family of four - and pay less than $100 in taxes per person! Screenshot from the Singapore Airlines website.

When redeeming KrisFlyer miles far enough in advance, you can even sometimes get a choice of flight times.

This kind of premium cabin award availability to Europe is exceptionally rare within the Qantas Frequent Flyer program. (Qantas also charges much higher taxes & charges than Singapore Airlines!)

## Overseas programs give you access to unique airline rewards

You can redeem Qantas and Velocity points to fly on many different partner airlines... if there are award seats available.

The majority of Australians use the Qantas Frequent Flyer program by default. Many Aussies also collect Velocity Frequent Flyer points. The problem with this is that there is only a limited pool of Classic Flight Reward seats available to book using Qantas points, or Reward Seats available to Velocity members.

Since so many Australians collect points in those two programs, there can be a LOT of competition for that limited pool of reward seats. If you want to use your points to fly overseas in a premium cabin, it can sometimes feel like you're competing with half of Australia for the same seats.

But if you have points with a different frequent flyer program, you can access award seats on airlines that aren't available to book using Qantas or Velocity points. This not only gives you more variety to choose from, but also makes it easier to find reward availability to and from Australia since you're competing with fewer people!

For example, you can often find lie-flat Business Class flights available to book using points on Air Mauritius. But this airline doesn't partner with any Australian programs - instead, you could book with Air Canada Aeroplan points.

Example of an Air Mauritius award flight available to book through Aeroplan on Air Canada's website.

Luckily, as we'll cover in Course 6, it's relatively easy to buy Aeroplan points at a discount. And the cost of buying Aeroplan points to redeem for a Business Class reward seat can be significantly less than the retail price of the seat you're redeeming for!

## Lower fuel surcharges

When you redeem frequent flyer points for a flight, you'll still need to cover any third-party fees and taxes out of your own pocket. Unfortunately, some airlines also add extra fuel surcharges, also known as "carrier charges", which go directly to the airline.

But not all frequent flyer programs pass these on!

For example, if you redeem Qantas points to fly Emirates from Melbourne to Singapore, as of November 2023, you could expect to pay between AU$325-$1,223 one-way in additional taxes & carrier charges, depending on your class of travel:

Emirates Classic Rewards available for booking using Qantas points on the Qantas website. Note the additional taxes & carrier charges!

But you could also use Aeroplan points to book a seat the same Emirates flight, and pay just CAD119 (~AU$134) of taxes & charges in all cabins. Aeroplan strikes again!

Emirates award availability from Melbourne to Singapore on the Air Canada website. Note the additional taxes & charges when booking through Aeroplan.

Some overseas programs even waive the carrier charges when using points to book Qantas flights - and charge fewer points, too!

For example, when redeeming through Qantas Frequent Flyer, it would cost 82,000 Qantas Points + $356 for a one-way Business Classic Reward seat from Sydney to Honolulu:

An example of Classic Reward seats from Sydney to Honolulu on the Qantas website.

But you could book exactly the same Business Class seat using points or miles with a Oneworld partner airline such as Alaska Airlines. In this case, the same seat would cost 75,000 Alaska Airlines Mileage Plan miles and only USD107 (approximately AU$158) in fees & taxes:

A Qantas Business reward from Sydney to Honolulu available to book with Alaska Mileage Plan miles on the Alaska Airlines website.

So, those are a few benefits of using overseas frequent flyer programs. But how can you *accumulate* points with overseas airlines if you live in Australia?

## Diversify your points using credit cards

Many Australian credit cards are co-branded Qantas Frequent Flyer cards. These cards exclusively earn Qantas points.

Rather than earning Qantas points, you could opt for a credit card that earns points in a credit card rewards program such as American Express Membership Rewards, NAB Rewards or St.George Amplify.

Most banks let you transfer their reward points to a variety of different airlines, including overseas airlines. This way, you'll retain the option to transfer your points to the frequent flyer program that best meets your needs at a later time. So, you have more options and you're not stuck holding points with just one airline. It's the ultimate insurance policy for points!

Credit cards are the easiest way to (indirectly) earn large amounts of points or miles in Australia with programs like KrisFlyer, Cathay and Qatar Airways Privilege Club. This is especially the case if you can take advantage of generous credit card sign-up bonuses!

## Other common ways to earn points with overseas airlines

Of course, each frequent flyer program has different partners and its own ways to earn points. But here are a few other common strategies to earn points with overseas airlines if you live in Australia...

## Fly on a partner airline

Although Qantas and Virgin Australia are the only major Australian airlines with frequent flyer programs, you can credit your points earned from Qantas and Virgin flights to partner airlines.

For example, if you fly with Qantas, you can still earn points and status credits (or the partner program's equivalent of those) with any Oneworld airline. So, you could fly Qantas and earn American Airlines AAdvantage miles or British Airways Executive Club Avios.

You can earn miles with American Airlines for flying Qantas, and vice versa. Photo: Qantas.

Likewise, when flying Virgin Australia, you could be earning points with Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer, Air Canada Aeroplan, Qatar Airways Privilege Club, Etihad Guest or United MileagePlus.

When crediting a flight to a partner airline, beware that some fare classes may be ineligible to earn points with the partner program. You can check your options using the Where To Credit website.

## Hotel stays

Most hotel loyalty programs provide the option to transfer your points to a range of airline frequent flyer programs.

You can also earn frequent flyer points by booking hotels through that airline's hotel booking platform (such as Alaska Airlines Hotels) or a platform like Rocketmiles. Rocketmiles lets you earn points for hotel bookings with your choice of many different airlines.

## Car rentals

Most major car rental companies (Hertz, Thrifty, Avis, Europcar, etc.) partner with a large range of airlines, allowing you to earn points on car rental bookings.

When renting a hire vehicle, you can often choose to credit the points to a wide range of loyalty programs. Photo: Matt Graham.

## Online shopping

Many frequent flyer programs let you earn points by shopping online through their online malls. Instead of clicking through to your preferred online retailer from the Qantas Shopping Online Mall or Velocity eStore, you could earn miles with United Airlines or Cathay Pacific.

You can earn United MileagePlus miles in Australia by shopping through the MileagePlus Shopping portal, or earn Cathay Asia Miles using Cathay Pacific's shopping portal.

## Buy points

Many frequent flyer programs also sell their points or miles to the public. When buying points, you'll often pay an inflated price if buying at full price... but some programs offer regular discounts or bonuses. This can actually work out to be quite lucrative in some cases.

We'll cover the strategy of buying points in Course 6.

Even if your frequent flyer program doesn't sell points, you may be able to buy points from a hotel loyalty program such as Marriott Bonvoy instead. You could then transfer your hotel points into your preferred airline points.
