# Air Canada Aeroplan

**Air Canada Aeroplan**

Air Canada sells its Aeroplan points with regular bonuses or discounts available. You can use Aeroplan points to fly with any Star Alliance airline, as well as many other interesting partner airlines, with competitive redemption rates and no fuel surcharges.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Why buy Aeroplan points?

The first overseas frequent flyer program we're going to look at is Air Canada's excellent Aeroplan program.

A member of Star Alliance, Air Canada began selling its Aeroplan points in May 2020. This opens up some great opportunities due to the sheer range of partner airlines that you can redeem Aeroplan points to fly with.

Air Canada is a member of Star Alliance. Photo: Matt Graham.

Aeroplan has relatively competitive flight reward charts, and it doesn't pass on any airline fuel or carrier surcharges - even when redeeming Aeroplan points to fly Emirates. So by buying points during a promotion and redeeming for Business & First Class reward flights on partner airlines, you can get some very good value.

Air Canada relaunched Aeroplan in November 2020 with brand new award charts and changes to reward flight pricing & flexibility. Reward flights operated by Air Canada are priced dynamically (within a certain range of points), but partner airline awards retain competitive fixed pricing.

With Aeroplan, you can also add a stopover to most one-way awards for 5,000 points. Most awards can be easily booked on the Air Canada website - which is probably a good thing, since it can take a while to get through to the Aeroplan call centre.

## Buying Aeroplan points

Air Canada sells Aeroplan points for CAD37.50 (~AU$41) per thousand at full price. This price excludes tax, but this doesn't apply to Australian residents.

You can purchase points in increments of 1,000, with higher increments applying to purchases above 30,000 points. There is a minimum of 2,000 points and maximum of 250,000 points per transaction (although this limit is routinely increased during special promotions). An annual purchase limit of 1,000,000 Aeroplan points applies per account, excluding bonus points.

Aeroplan runs promotional offers on buying points every 1-3 months. The highest ever amount of bonus points offered was 125% during July 2024. Typical offers give around 60-100% bonus points.

Beware that **Aeroplan points expiry after 18 months of account inactivity**.

You can buy, transfer or reinstate points on the Aeroplan website. Once purchased, points may take up to 72 hours to post to your Aeroplan account.

## How to book

It is possible to book most Aeroplan awards on the Air Canada website. Simply search for flights from the home page, but select the "Book with points AEROPLAN" box:

Select "Book with points" to search for Aeroplan award flights on the Air Canada website.

To add a stopover when booking online, select the "Multi-city/Stopover" option.

If you cannot find reward availability on the Aeroplan website, we recommend searching for seats on the United Airlines website (for Star Alliance awards) or the Etihad Guest/American Airlines websites (for Etihad Airways awards).

If reward seats on Air Canada's partner airlines are showing as available elsewhere, try calling Aeroplan's Contact Centre on 1800 361 5373 (US/Canada) or +1 514 395 0300 (international number) between 7am and midnight (US/Canadian Eastern Time). You may also need to call to book award seats on partner airlines that are unavailable online.

There is a CAD30 fee for Aeroplan Contact Centre bookings, even for bookings that can't be made online.

When redeeming Aeroplan points, you don't necessarily have to have the full amount of points already in your account. There may be an option to purchase top-up points online during the booking process if you're a little short!

For more information on using the Air Canada website to search for award flights, see the video demonstration in our "Find Star Alliance Reward Availability" unit in Course 5.

## Aeroplan reward charts

When redeeming Aeroplan points **for Air Canada, United, Etihad Airways, Emirates or Flydubai** flights, as well as a selection of regional Canadian airlines (or itineraries including flights on Air Canada), **dynamic pricing** applies. This means the number of points you’ll need varies, depending on how much demand there is for that flight. 

For Air Canada, this means that all seats are available for redemption on all Air Canada flights. The price will be within the range outlined in the new Aeroplan award charts.

For other partners, it does mean more award seats are available, including Etihad Business and First Class awards. Pricing begins at a minimum level, although it can often be much higher.

Aeroplan redemptions on all other **partner airlines** (including itineraries that include multiple other partner airlines) cost a **fixed amount of points**. These amounts are also outlined in the Aeroplan flight rewards charts. These are typically better value than reward flights on the dynamically-priced partners.

You can get a great deal on EgyptAir via Aeroplan, or you can pay over three times as much for an Emirates flight. Screenshot from the Air Canada website.

In the Aeroplan reward charts, the world is divided into four travel zones:

Travel zones in the new Aeroplan flight reward chart

The cost of an award will be based on the zone/s **and** the total distance travelled. In addition to using Aeroplan’s award charts, you can check how many points a reward flight is likely to cost using Air Canada's Points Predictor tool.

There are **no fuel or carrier surcharges when redeeming Aeroplan points** on any airline! You’ll just pay airport & government taxes, and there is a fixed CAD39 (~AU$44) partner reward booking fee for bookings including travel on partner airlines.

## Sweet spots

Here are some of the Aeroplan flight reward chart sweet spots of interest (note that prices quoted are one-way):

- Flights with the "Pacific" zone, which includes Australia, New Zealand, South-East Asia & North Asia are very reasonably priced in Business & First Class.

- Flights between Australia and Europe or Africa are good value in Business & First class. You can fly in any direction – even via North America. For example, Business Class flights from Australia to Europe, Africa or the Seychelles cost 110,000 points. First Class is 140,000 points.

- Flights from Australia to Asia (e.g. Melbourne-Bangkok with Thai Airways) cost from 45,000 points in Business Class.

- Most flights from Australia to North America cost 75,000-87,500 points in Business Class. You can add a stopover in Asia for 5,000 points.

- Most flights from South Africa to Europe are just 60,000 points in Business Class.

- Combine two or three one-way awards with stopovers to build a round-the-world itinerary (see below).

## Round-the-world awards

Air Canada no longer offers Star Alliance round-the-world award tickets via Aeroplan. However, you can book a mini-RTW trip by getting creative with multiple one-way mileage redemptions and taking advantage of stopovers.

For example, you could book three separate award tickets in Business class:

- Sydney to Bangkok with a stopover in Singapore, flying Singapore Airlines, for 50,000 Aeroplan points (45,000 points for a partner airline award covering up to 5,000 miles within the Pacific zone, plus 5,000 points for the stopover in Singapore)

- Delhi to New York with a stopover in Frankfurt, flying Lufthansa or Air India/United, for 95,000 Aeroplan points (90,000 points for a partner airline award covering up to 8,000 miles between the Atlantic & North America zones, plus 5,000 points for the stopover in Frankfurt)

- Los Angeles to Sydney, flying United, for 75,000 Aeroplan points (this is the cost of the lowest-priced award flight on United up to 7,500 miles in length between the North America & Pacific zones)

By adding on cheap one-way positioning flights from Bangkok-Delhi and New York-Los Angeles, you would have created a round-the-world trip in Business Class, flying on Aeroplan partner airlines, for just 220,000 points + minimal taxes & fees.

This is just one simple example - many other variations of round-the-world itineraries are possible!

## Award routing rules

Aeroplan has fairly generous and flexible award routing rules. Key routing rules include:

- One stopover of up to 45 days can be added to any one-way award for an additional 5,000 Aeroplan points, excluding within Canada or USA (on round-trip awards, you could add two stopovers for 10,000 points)

- There is generally no maximum permitted mileage or draconian routing restrictions - if there is award availability, you can book the flights

- Open-jaws are permitted

- Mixed class bookings are permitted (you'll pay the rate of the highest class flown in)

For a full list of Aeroplan award routing rules, Air Canada has helpfully publishing a Flight Reward Policy on its website.

## Fees on Aeroplan reward bookings

The following fees apply to Aeroplan rewards:

- Cancellation fee: CAD150 online or CAD175 via the Aeroplan Contact Centre (up to 2 hours before departure)

- Change fee: CAD75 per direction or CAD100 if change is made within 60 days of travel

- You may change or cancel Aeroplan rewards at no cost within 24 hours of booking

## Summary: Buying Aeroplan points

- Air Canada Aeroplan points cost CAD37.50 per thousand

- Look out for bonus point offers

- Aeroplan points can be redeemed for flights on Star Alliance airlines + Etihad Airways, Air Serbia, Air Mauritius, Virgin Australia & other partners

- Points are best used for flights to Europe, North America, the Middle East or Asia

- Award routing rules are very flexible and stopovers can be added for 5,000 points
