# Exchanging Cash at the Best Rate

**Exchanging Cash at the Best Rate**

Don't get ripped off on foreign currency exchange! This unit will show you how to get the best rate.

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Using cash overseas

Even if you plan to pay for most overseas purchases by card, you'll probably still need a little bit of cash when travelling for smaller purchases. Depending on the country, not every business will accept credit card payments.

There are some countries, such as Sweden, Norway, Switzerland and to an extent Australia, where you can probably get by without any cash. But cash is still king in many parts of the world. Even some supermarkets in Germany and the Netherlands still don't accept foreign credit cards.

By paying with cash, you may even get a better price on transactions that involve bargaining.

Cash is still king in many parts of the world.

There are three main ways to obtain foreign currency:

- Exchanging cash in Australia

- Exchanging cash overseas

- ATM withdrawal method

In most cases, **the ATM withdrawal method is both the easiest and cheapest way to obtain cash in foreign currency**. This involves using one of the debit cards featured in the previous unit to withdraw cash from an overseas ATM.

## How banks make money on foreign currency exchange

There are two ways that banks make money on foreign currency exchange:

- Charging commission/fees for processing the transaction

- Adding a margin on the exchange rate used, known as "spread"

When the bank buys or sells foreign currency, they'll be doing so at the official "spot rate". But there can be a substantial difference between the spot rate and the final rate passed on to the customer. This is the bank's margin.

Some banks charge no commission on currency exchange. They can afford to do this because of the margin they're making on the actual exchange rate.

Therefore, you need to keep both the commission and the exchange rate into account when making comparisons. The easiest way to compare rates is to calculate how much of a foreign currency you'll receive in exchange for the same amount in Australian dollars.

When exchanging money, check the spread. This is the difference between the bank's "buy" and "sell" rates for the same currency.

## Exchanging cash in Australia

If you need to change cash before you leave Australia, there are a variety of places where you can do this. But the rate you'll get can vary significantly!

Based on our research, here are your options from worst to best value:

The best place to change your money is somewhere that charges a low margin and no commission. Speciality foreign exchange outlets like Crown or United Currency Exchange do just that! These outlets tend to offer the greatest overall value.

The rates offered by Australia Post are usually not *quite *as good as these specialty outlets. But the rates are generally better than those at all four of the major banks. Plus, Australia Post charges no commission. If you don't live in a major city, Australia Post may offer the best combination of value and convenience.

The banks tend to charge higher margins on their exchange rates. They also charge relatively high commissions!

But the worst place to change money, hands down, is at the airport. The exchange rates at airport outlets such as Travelex are consistently terrible, and the commissions are high. When we conducted a spot check of the exchange rate available at a Travelex airport location at Sydney Airport, we found that **you would receive around 10% less cash than you would by exchanging at a speciality outlet found in the CBDs of most major Australian cities.**

Having said that, **you *can* get a reasonable rate when changing money at the airport if you plan ahead**! Travelex offers much better rates, and no commission, to customers that pre-purchase online. When we checked, there was only a 2.1% difference between the exchange rate of a CBD specialty outlet and the pre-paid Travelex airport rate.

## Exchanging cash overseas

Depending on the country, the exchange rates in your destination country may be more competitive than in Australia. So if you don't need cash immediately on arrival, waiting to exchange overseas may be a better option.

The same principles of looking for outlets with no commissions and a low exchange rate spread apply.

## ATM withdrawal method

The cheapest way to obtain foreign currency is actually not to exchange cash at all!

Instead of changing cash at a traditional outlet, we highly recommend using the ATM withdrawal method. ATMs are located in most international airport terminals, so you can **withdraw the cash you need as soon as you arrive in the country**. Plus, by using the right card, you can **get a better exchange rate and pay no fees**!

Lineup of ATM cash machines in the arrivals hall of Astana International Airport. Photo: Matt Graham.

The previous unit features a range of debit cards which charge no fees on overseas ATM withdrawals. Every Australian travelling overseas should have at least one of these cards!

There are three possible fees that may be charged when you withdraw cash from an overseas ATM:

- The ATM operator may charge a withdrawal fee (usually displayed at the time of the transaction if applicable)

- Your own bank may charge an overseas ATM withdrawal fee

- Your own bank may charge an international transaction fee

By using a fee-free debit card and an ATM with no operator fees, you can avoid all of these fees! Plus, you'll normally get a far better exchange rate than you would at a bank.

## Wise

If you need to transfer money to an overseas bank account, Wise (formerly TransferWise) is one of the easiest *and* best value methods to do this.

With Wise, you can easily transfer money online. Rather than hiding their fees in the exchange rate, Wise uses the foreign currency "spot rate" and just charges a small, transparent fee for each transaction.

The fees vary depending on your payment method (you can use bank transfer, POLi, PayID, debit card or credit card) but can be as low as 0.51% per transfer - which is significantly better than you'll get from any of the major banks or PayPal!

When using Wise, your recipient will also receive their money relatively quickly. In fact, for over 60% of transfers, it takes less than a minute.

If you need to transfer money overseas, Wise is a no-brainer! Wise also offers a multi-currency bank account with low fees, which could be another good option if you're looking for a travel money card or bank account.

If paying for Wise transfers with a credit card, beware that your bank may treat the transaction as a cash advance. This means your bank may charge immediate interest and/or not award frequent flyer points. Therefore, we suggest using another payment method (e.g. bank transfer) when transferring cash with Wise.

As an alternative to Wise, Revolut offers a similar service.

## Keep an eye on exchange rates

If you're planning to make a large overseas purchase or transfer, keep an eye on exchange rates. These fluctuate all the time.

You'll get better bang for your buck by paying in foreign currency when the Australian dollar is high.
