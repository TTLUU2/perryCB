# American Airlines AAdvantage

**American Airlines AAdvantage**

American Airlines AAdvantage miles can open up cheap premium cabin travel on Qantas & oneworld airlines to New Zealand, Asia, Europe and even around Australia.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Why buy AAdvantage miles?

American AAdvantage miles open the door to cut-price travel in Business and First Class to many destinations, including Asia, Europe and the Pacific Islands!

American Airlines is a founding member of the Oneworld alliance. AAdvantage miles can be redeemed to fly on all Oneworld airlines, including Qantas, as well as partners like Etihad Airways. This provides plenty of opportunities to redeem AAdvantage miles.

American Airlines is part of the Oneworld alliance. Photo: Qantas.

American Airlines regularly offers generous bonus miles, and the cost of award tickets is low. American has a competitive zone-based award chart for travel on partner airlines with some great sweet spots.

There are also **no fuel surcharges** when redeeming AAdvantage miles on most airlines - even Qantas! The only exceptions are flights on British Airways, Iberia and China Southern, which do have fuel surcharges passed on.

Note that American Airlines AAdvantage miles expire if there is no activity in your account for more than 18 months.

## Buying AAdvantage miles

American Airlines AAdvantage miles become cheaper as you buy more of them. Prices typically start at USD75.25 for 2,000 miles (including a 7.5% U.S. Federal Excise tax), although pricing may vary between members. The price-per-thousand decreases as you purchase more miles, and you can purchase miles in increments of 1,000.

However, you would rarely pay full price for miles! AAdvantage promotions are frequent and up to 100% bonus miles have been offered. Some promotions even include a 10% discount on miles, which is on top of the bonus.

AAdvantage also has an evergreen promotion of up to 35% off miles, if you purchase the maximum amount.

There is an annual purchase limit of 150,000 AAdvantage miles (excluding bonus miles).

Buying miles from American Airlines is a relatively simple process. Purchases should post to your account instantly but may take up to 8 hours.

You can purchase miles on the American Airlines website.

## How to book

Awards on the following airlines can be booked on the American Airlines website:

- All Oneworld airlines

- Air Tahiti Nui

- Etihad Airways

- Fiji Airways

- GOL Airlines

- Hawaiian Airlines

To book travel including partner airlines that don't appear online - or for other itineraries that aren't showing up online - call American Airlines on **02 9101 1948 **from within Australia. (See the American Airlines website for other global contact numbers.)

## Paying the taxes with a non-US credit card

When booking a ticket on the American Airlines website, you'll need to enter the address associated with your credit card. For some reason, the website will give you an error message if you enter an address outside of the USA.

If you don't have a US credit card, you can get around this by entering your real street address but inputing the country as "United States". You'll need to provide a state and ZIP code; the AFF community suggests using your Australian postcode but adding a zero to the front to make it five digits. Then, Google which US state this ZIP code corresponds to and just list that as your state. This is an annoying workaround, but it generally works.

## AAdvantage award charts

The number of miles required for a particular redemption varies by route, with rates based on geographical zones. Flights within Australia, regardless of distance, cost a fixed number of miles. This means that longer domestic flights are a great value redemption. There are numerous other sweet spots, such as Business class flights to Asia.

The AAdvantage flight redemption tables are split into separate charts for redemptions on American Airlines and on other partner airlines. Of interest to most Australians is the oneworld & partner airline award chart.

Note that American Airlines appears to be moving to dynamic award pricing for travel that includes flights on American Airlines metal. But pricing for partner airline awards remains consistent.

## Sweet spots

There are numerous sweet spots in the program:

- Business and First Class flights from Australia to Asia are excellent value

- Longer domestic flights remain good value, as awards from anywhere in Australia to anywhere else in Australia cost the same number of points

- Trans-Tasman Business class redemptions are reasonable value, especially for longer routes like Perth-Auckland or Broome-Queenstown

- Business and First Class flights from Australia to Europe are good value

- First Class flights from Japan or South Korea to the USA are good value

- Business & First Class flights on Etihad Airways/Qatar Airways from Australia to Abu Dhabi/Doha are good value, and availability can usually be found if you are flexible

- Some of the redemption zones, e.g. Africa, Europe, Asia Region 2 and the Middle East/Indian subcontinent are notably large. Intra-zone redemptions could be a sweet spot here as they are inexpensive and could potentially cover a very large distance if maximised.

## AAdvantage award routing rules

Key American Airlines award routing rules:

- One-way redemptions are allowed with AAdvantage, and cost no extra compared to booking return flights

- No stopovers (of greater than 24 hours) are allowed on award bookings

- The route must be a published routing (i.e. no backtracking unless required by the airline's route network)

- Finally, it is not generally permitted to travel through a third "zone" in order to reach your final destination. If you need to do this, you may need to purchase two separate awards. For example, it's not possible to book an award flight from Australia to the USA via Asia, for the same cost as going direct. You'd need to redeem miles for two flights; one from Australia to Asia and one from Asia to the USA. There are some exceptions to this. For example, you may travel from Australia to Europe via Abu Dhabi on Etihad or via Doha on Qatar Airways.

## Award booking fees

American Airlines no longer changes fees for award bookings and changes, nor for booking over the phone.

## Summary: Buying AAdvantage miles

- Miles become cheaper as you buy more; as a rough guide the price is around USD32 per thousand at full price

- Promotions are very frequent and a typical offer is 60% bonus miles

- AAdvantage miles can be redeemed on Oneworld airlines and various other partners including Etihad and Fiji Airways

- Miles are best used for premium cabin travel to Asia, Europe, trans-Tasman and long domestic flights within Australia
