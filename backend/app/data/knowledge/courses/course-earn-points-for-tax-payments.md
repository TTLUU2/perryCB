# Earn Points for Tax Payments

**Earn Points for Tax Payments**

Maximise your points by paying the Australian Tax Office (ATO) with a credit card. Paying tax has never been so rewarding!

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Earning points for tax payments

You can earn points for *almost* any purchase made with a credit card. So, using your credit card to pay for as much as you possibly can is a great way to maximise your points!

Many Australian businesses will have to pay tax to the Australian Tax Office (ATO) at some point. Some Australians will also need to pay off their HECS/HELP debt. If you're paying money to the ATO anyway, why not earn points on these payments?

Unfortunately, most Australian banks no longer award frequent flyer points when you use your credit card for government payments, such as to the ATO or when paying council rates.

But **there are still some credit cards that do earn points for ATO payments**. Plus, there are now lots of **bill payment services** such as pay.com.au that you can use to pay tax (or any other bills) and **still earn a full suite of frequent flyer points** - regardless of which credit card you're using!

In general, when paying tax by credit card, you'll need to pay a small payment surcharge. This is payable either directly to the ATO, or to the bill payment service that you're using. The trade-off is that you'll earn valuable points that you can redeem for flights, upgrades or anything else that takes your fancy!

Turn your bill payments into Business Class flights! Photo: Qantas.

If you have a large tax bill, you could be earning many thousands of points every year! But paying tax by credit card can be a useful way to earn some extra points even when paying smaller amounts.

## How to pay the ATO by credit card

If you wish pay the ATO by credit card directly, you can use the Government *EasyPay* portal. Visa, Mastercard and American Express (Amex) cards are accepted. You can pay any amount between $10 and $20 million at a time.

Alternatively, if you wish to use a bill payment service, you'll need to pay via their platform (see below).

## ATO card payment fees

There is a surcharge for credit card payments that is equal to merchant fee paid by the ATO. This fee is effectively the cost of the points you'll be receiving for the transaction.

The exact surcharge depends on the type of credit card you're using. At the time of writing, the ATO card payment fees are:

- Australian Mastercard credit cards - 0.72%

- Australian Visa credit cards - 0.8%

- Amex cards - 1.45%

Different surcharges apply to international cards and the rates are subject to change. There are no fees for Visa and Mastercard debit card payments. See the full list of current rates on the ATO website.

## Card payments as a tax deduction

The card payment fee may be tax-deductible if you are paying a business tax liability. The ATO website has more details about deductions for tax-related operating expenses.

Please note that this information is general in nature only. You may wish to consult your account or seek professional financial advice relevant to your circumstances. Also consider that there could be possible Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) implications for earning very large quantities of points via tax payments.

## Which credit cards award points for tax payments?

Banks generally make little or no profit when awarding points for ATO payments. That's why most banks now specifically exclude government transactions from earning points. But some Australian credit cards - mostly business cards and American Express cards - *do* still award points for tax payments.

## Australian bill payment services

If your credit card does not award points for ATO payments, you can get around this by using one of a growing number of Australian business-to-business payment platforms.

These services allow you to pay bills to the ATO or other businesses using your credit card. You'll have to pay a service/transaction fee to the payment service provider, but you'll **earn points for your credit card spend at the full everyday earn rate** applicable to your credit card.

In addition to any points earned through your credit card, some payment services award bonus points directly through their platforms. For example, you could earn:

- 1 Qantas Point per $7 paid to the ATO through B2Bpay

- 1 Velocity or Qantas Point per $10 paid to the ATO through Yak Pay or Payment Logic (no bonus points are awarded if you choose a Visa plan with Custom Pricing)

- 1 Velocity Point per $10 paid through Sniip

- Pay.com.au provides the option to purchase a package that includes extra PayRewards Points as part of each payment.

PayRewards points are transferrable to:

- Qantas Business Rewards

- Virgin Australia Business Flyer

- Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer

- Qatar Airways Privilege Club

- Air Canada Aeroplan

- Cathay

- Vietnam Airlines Lotusmiles

- Accor Live Limitless

- Marriott Bonvoy

- IHG One Rewards

These services are generally intended for business payments, and you may need an Australian Business Number (ABN) to create an account.

Payments may take several days to process when using an intermediary. But most of these services also offer some useful features to assist with business cash flow, such as the ability to schedule payments.

When using a bill payment service, you'll generally have to pay a higher fee than you would when paying your tax directly to the ATO (either by credit card or using BPAY). The trade-off is that you may be able to earn points for your payment at a much higher rate.

## Which services are the best value?

As you can see, each of these services has a slightly different fee structure. Many also exclude GST (which adds 10% to the cost) in the quoted fee amount.

On the surface, it could appear that Easy Bill Pay has the lowest fees. But this doesn’t account for their $24.95 monthly account fee, or their 0.75% "High Value Processing Fee" surcharge on payments over $20,000 in a calendar month. This reduces the value proposition of Easy Bill Pay significantly.

With pay.com.au, the amount paid depends on your membership level. Free members pay 1% + GST for Mastercard payments, but this reduces to 0.9% + GST for Regular members and 0.8% + GST for Premium members. Regular or Premium membership of pay.com.au costs $85 or $165 per month, respectively.

With this taken into account, the best value options would generally be:

- pay.com.au for Visa payments

- Yak Pay for Mastercard payments

- For American Express payments, this would depend on your transaction amounts and any memberships you have with payment services

If you want to pay a personal bill to a business that is not the ATO, Sniip's 1.29% rate for Amex payments (including GST) or Payment Logic Personal's rate of 1.25% (including GST) is great. But these rates can't be used for tax payments.

**An example of the value:**

John uses his Citi Prestige Qantas credit card to pay a $10,000 ATO bill via a payment platform that charges a 1% fee for Mastercard payments. He would pay a $100 fee to the payment platform (which may be tax-deductible) and would earn 10,100 Qantas Points through his credit card.

John may also be able to earn extra points by paying off the credit card balance via bank transfer using a platform such as Yak Pay or pay.com.au.

## Risks

There are some risks involved with paying your tax by credit card:

- Banks may stop awarding points on ATO payments at any time, including without warning.

- If you are using a personal credit card, there is a risk this could be cancelled if you put very large business expenses (including tax payments) on the card. To avoid this, choose a business credit card or use your personal card for a variety of other expenses as well - not just ATO payments.

- You should discuss any potential Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) implications with your accountant.
