# Qantas Frequent Flyer Status

**Qantas Frequent Flyer Status**

Many Australians aspire to earn Qantas Frequent Flyer status. This unit explains the benefits and how to earn it.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Qantas Frequent Flyer status tiers

All Qantas Frequent Flyer members begin as Bronze members. You can upgrade to Silver, Gold, Platinum and Platinum One status by earning status credits from flying.

While Silver status comes with a few perks, the real benefits (such as airport lounge access) kick in when you reach Gold status. Many frequent flyers consider Gold status to be the "sweet spot" in terms of the benefits received, compared to the amount of flying needed to earn it.

It takes around twice as much flying to earn Platinum status, compared to Gold status. However, Qantas Platinum status does come with some magnificent extra benefits including First Lounge access and the ability to request extra Classic Reward seats.

Here's an overview of the key benefits of each tier:

**Qantas Silver (Oneworld Ruby):**

- 1x complimentary Qantas Club pass

- Priority check-in

- Extra baggage allowance

- 50% more points on flights

**Qantas Gold (Oneworld Sapphire):**

- Access to domestic Qantas Club and international Business Lounges

- Priority boarding and baggage

- 75% bonus points on flights

**Qantas Platinum (Oneworld Emerald):**

- International First Class Lounge and domestic Business Lounge access

- Ability to request additional Classic Reward seats

- Higher upgrade priority

- Call centre priority

- 100% bonus points on flights

**Qantas Platinum One (Oneworld Emerald):**

- Access to the VIP Service Team

- Free exit row seats

- Most change fees waived

- Gift Platinum status to your partner

## Qantas status qualification requirements

Every Qantas Frequent Flyer member has their own personal "membership year". Yours will end on the last day of the month in which you joined the program.

Once you achieve the required number of status credits for a status upgrade, you'll hold that status level for the rest of your membership year **and a full year after that**.

At the end of each membership year, your status credits will reset to zero. To renew your status, you'll need to earn enough status credits again in the following membership year.

You can generally only earn status credits by flying. There are a few exceptions, such as limited-time Qantas Frequent Flyer partner promotions or credit cards offering a small amount of status credits as a one-off sign-up bonus.

## Lifetime Qantas status

In addition, Qantas offers lifetime status based on your total lifetime status credit tally:

- Lifetime Silver - 7,000 lifetime status credits

- Lifetime Gold - 14,000 lifetime status credits

- Lifetime Platinum - 75,000 lifetime status credits

Lifetime Gold is the "sweet spot" here, being reasonably achievable while offering worthwhile benefits. Lifetime Platinum requires 75,000 lifetime status credits, which is extremely high and not worth aiming for in most cases.

## Loyalty Bonuses

You'll earn a Loyalty Bonus of 50 status credits for every 500 status credits earned from Qantas & Jetstar flights per membership year, up to 4 times per year.

In addition, if you reach 2,400 status credits within a single membership year, you'll be able to choose a Platinum Bonus Reward. This gives you a choice of:

- 50,000 bonus Qantas points, or

- Gift a complimentary Gold membership

## Earning status credits

In addition to the points, status credits are earned when flying on:

- Any Qantas flight

- Any Qantas marketed codeshare flight with a "QF" flight number that's operated by a partner airline

- Any Jetstar flight with the purchase of a Flex, Flex Plus or Business Max Bundle (no points or status credits are earned on Starter or Starter Plus fares)

- Any flight marketed and operated by a Oneworld airline, provided you've booked a fare class that's eligible for earning

You generally won't earn status credits when flying with a non-Oneworld partner airline unless you've booked a Qantas-marketed flight with a "QF" code.

The number of status credits earned will depend on the route, airline and fare class. See the charts on the Qantas website to check the current earn rates.

## Opportunities for bonus status credits

Qantas typically runs "double status credits" promotions around 1-2 times per year. Booking during one of these promotions can be lucrative for obvious reasons.

Qantas Premier Titanium cardholders can also earn an extra 20% on eligible Qantas flights paid for using that credit card.
