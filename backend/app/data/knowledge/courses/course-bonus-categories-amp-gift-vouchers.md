# Bonus Categories &amp; Gift Vouchers

**Bonus Categories & Gift Vouchers**

Earn even more points from your everyday spend, or save money on your groceries, by using your credit card to purchase gift cards.

## Bonus categories for credit card spend

Some credit cards award bonus points if you spend in certain categories. For example, many co-brand Qantas Frequent Flyer credit cards will award an extra point per dollar spent on Qantas flights.

Some credit cards even award points at a higher rate when shopping at supermarkets, petrol stations or department stores.

If you regularly shop at certain types of stores, it could be worth choosing a card that will reward you for that spend at a higher rate.

## Buying gift cards at supermarkets

If you have a credit card that earns you bonus points at supermarkets, it makes sense to spend as much as possible at retailers where you'll earn those bonus points!

One way to maximise your points is to purchase gift vouchers from supermarkets. You can then spend these vouchers at other retailers where you would otherwise earn fewer points. This method could also help you to save on credit card surcharges and use your American Express where it might not otherwise be accepted.

For example, you might wish to purchase a new computer from JB Hi-fi. Instead of earning only 1 point per dollar, you could purchase JB Hi-fi gift vouchers at Woolworths using your American Express card and earn extra points for the purchase. Then, simply redeem the vouchers for the computer at JB Hi-fi.

Note that some stores may require you to provide ID if paying for items using large numbers of gift cards.

## Woolworths & Coles often give bonus points on gift cards

To really maximise your points, you could even buy gift cards from Woolworths or Coles when the supermarkets are offering their own bonus points. For example, Coles often gives 10-20x Flybuys points (worth 5-10 Velocity points per $1 spent) when you buy certain types of gift cards.

You could put the payment through on your credit card to earn bonus points through your card, plus earn lots of Flybuys or Everyday Rewards points!

## Discounted Woolworths gift cards

There are numerous ways to purchase Woolworths Group gift cards at a 3-5% discount off the face value of the gift card.

In general, you can get a 3% discount off WISH gift cards which are valid at most Woolworths Group stores. But the discount on store-specific gift cards increases to 4% for **Woolworths**, and 5% for **Big W**, **BWS** or **Dan Murphy's** gift cards.

Many organisations offer access to these discounts to their members. This includes all of the state-based automobile associations (e.g. NRMA in NSW, RACV in Victoria, RACQ in Queensland, etc.) except for the ACT. You can also access these discounts via Entertainment Book or if you're a Macquarie Bank, HCF or Alinta Energy customer.

If you have a **NSW Seniors Card, **you can get 5% off WISH gift cards which can be redeemed at Woolworths, Big W, Dan Murphy's, BWS and Woolworths Caltex co-branded petrol outlets. You can also buy gift cards for other people.

You can create Woolworths gift cards for any amount between $5 and $500. There is no cost if you choose an "eGift Card" which is delivered online. You can then redeem your Wish gift cards at Woolworths for groceries or for other gift vouchers.

Woolworths gift card purchases also qualify as supermarket spend on your credit card. So you'll still earn any bonus points for supermarket spend.

## Meeting minimum spend requirements

Many credit card sign-up bonus offers require that you spend a minimum amount on the card within 3 months to earn the bonus points. This can be difficult if you don't have any large payments due in this period.

One way to artificially increase your spend is to purchase gift cards using your credit card. You can then redeem the vouchers later, but they'll be placed on your current credit card billing cycle. This trick works most of the time, but do check that your bank doesn't specifically exclude gift cards as eligible spend.

For more ideas on meeting credit card minimum spend requirements, see our training unit on bonus sign-up points.
