# Find Virgin Australia &amp; Velocity Partner Reward Availability

**Find Virgin Australia & Velocity Partner Reward Availability**

We show you how to find Reward Seat availability on Virgin Australia and its partner airlines.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Where to find award availability

Virgin Australia is not in an alliance, but it has a diverse range of international partner airlines from multiple alliances. Virgin also has partners that aren't in any alliance.

As a reminder, these are the airlines that you can redeem Virgin Australia's Velocity Frequent Flyer points to fly with:

While this does have some advantages, a disadvantage is that no single website displays award availability on all of Virgin Australia's partner airlines.

When redeeming Velocity points, you'll first need to identify which airline/s you wish to use. Then, search on the appropriate website accordingly...

## Virgin Australia website

You can generally find Velocity Reward Seat availability for the following airlines on the Virgin Australia website:

- Virgin Australia

- Air Canada

- All Nippon Airways

- Qatar Airways

- Singapore Airlines

- United Airlines

To book a reward flight using Velocity points on any of the airlines listed above, simply log into your Velocity Frequent Flyer account and search for flights on the home page of the Virgin Australia website. When entering your origin and destination, select the "Use Velocity Points" slider:

Check the "Use Velocity Points" slider on the Virgin Australia website.

On the results page, you may be presented with a range of different options. Be sure to select flights marked as "Reward", and not the more expensive "Any Seat" options shown on the right of the screen.

Look for "Reward" seats when redeeming Velocity points for flights on the Virgin Australia website. If it's not marked as a Reward Seat, it's probably a Points Plus Pay flight and isn't at the fixed Reward Seat price.

Note that flights on some partner airline routes may not appear on the Virgin Australia website. For example, Air Canada trans-Atlantic reward flights may only be bookable over the phone and the Virgin website doesn't recognise some destinations. You may need to use an alternative website in this case, or call the Velocity Membership Contact Centre.

You'll also need to call Velocity to book multi-city awards, mixed-cabin rewards or flights with long transits, as these can't be booked online.

## Airlines that aren't shown on the Virgin Australia website

For airlines that aren't shown on the Virgin Australia website at all, you can use alternative websites to search for reward availability before calling:

- **South African Airways:** Use a Star Alliance website such as United or Air Canada - see our unit on finding Star Alliance availability

- **Virgin Atlantic:** Use the Delta website, Air France/KLM website, ExpertFlyer or SeatSpy

- **Capital Airlines:** Search for award seats on the Hainan Airlines website (you need a Fortune Wings Club account)

- **Hainan Airlines:** Use ExpertFlyer or search for award seats on the Hainan Airlines website (you need a Fortune Wings Club account)

- **Hong Kong Airlines:** Search for award seats on the Hong Kong Airlines website (you need a Fortune Wings Club account)

To book a reward flight on Capital Airlines, Hong Kong Airlines, Hainan Airlines, South African Airways or Virgin Atlantic, you will need to call the Velocity Membership Contact Centre on 13 18 75 (within Australia), or +61 2 8667 5924 (from outside Australia). The call centre is open from Monday-Saturday.

**The United Airlines website can also be useful for finding Virgin Australia reward seat availability.** Unlike the Virgin website which only shows one day at a time, the United website shows award seats 5 weeks at a time. No login is required, but it is possible that United could have access to different reward seats than Velocity members.

You can use the United website to search more easily for Virgin Australia reward seat availability.
