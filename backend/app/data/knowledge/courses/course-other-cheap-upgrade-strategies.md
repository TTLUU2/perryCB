# Other Cheap Upgrade Strategies

**Other Cheap Upgrade Strategies**

There are more ways to score a cheap upgrade on your next flight! This unit looks at some of the other "left-field" ways to upgrade.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Optiontown upgrades

Another way to get an upgrade is through the Optiontown website's "Upgrade Travel Option".

Optiontown website.

Optiontown offers heavily discounted upgrades on a small handful of carriers that haven't yet switched to using Plusgrade. Airlines covered include:

- Vietnam Airlines (note - lounge access & additional baggage allowance not given on upgrades)

## How to request an Optiontown upgrade

Unfortunately, the process of upgrading with Optiontown is not particularly user-friendly. There is also some risk involved. But there *is *some exceptional value to be had. For example, Matt successfully upgraded to Business Class for just $32 using Optiontown.

For a pre-determined fee, you can request an upgrade to Business Class on any of Optiontown's participating airlines. There are various other upgrade options as well, such as a free neighbouring seat and lounge access.

EgyptAir Boeing 737-800 Business Class, used on shorter flights. Photo: Matt Graham.

You'll need to request the upgrade directly through Optiontown - not with the operating airline. To check how much the upgrade will cost, and to request one, simply enter your booking details on the Optiontown website.

## Payments to Optiontown

There are two payments involved with each upgrade request:

- A small, non-refundable "sign-up" fee. This is usually a few dollars.

- The cost of the upgrade.

You'll need to pay for both of these in advance, however the cost of the upgrade should be refunded within a week after the flight if it doesn't come through.

Again, success will depend on premium cabin availability. But if there are seats available and your upgrade is successful, you'll be notified any time from 72 to 4 hours prior to the flight.

Upgrade prices do vary, even for flights on the same route. It depends on availability. More availability in Business class will mean lower prices. But to give you an idea of the potential value, Premium Flatbed upgrades on AirAsia X flights between Kuala Lumpur and Australia may be possible for less than $200!

Beware that there are some reviews online from people that had trouble receiving a refund if their upgrade was unsuccessful. If you don't receive the upgrade, do make sure you get your money back. If you don't, you could try requesting a credit card chargeback.

## Discounted upgrades at check-in

If there are still free business class seats on the day of your flight, some airlines will try to sell them for a discount at check in!

Sometimes there will be a sign on the check-in counter with an upgrade price list. At other times, you may need to ask the check-in agent politely if a paid upgrade is available. The prices charged by each airline will vary.

To be clear, simply asking for a free upgrade is unlikely to win you any favours. It is unrealistic to expect a free upgrade just because you asked for one, and the staff won't have any authority to give you one anyway. But the airline may be prepared to sell you one at a discounted rate. When asking at the check-in counter, you should make it clear that you're requesting a paid upgrade, and not a free one.

## Discounted on-board upgrades

If you've ever walked past an empty Business Class seat - wishing you could sit there as you made your way to the cramped economy cabin - there's another option for you! Some airlines will even let you escape to the comfort of a higher cabin *after *you've boarded the plane.

British Airways, China Southern, AirAsia X and Lufthansa are a few of the airlines known to offer on-board paid upgrades. In the case of Lufthansa, an announcement is made after take-off offering vacant Premium Economy seats to Economy passengers for a "special price".

Emirates, Air France and KLM will even let you upgrade after boarding using the airline's own miles.
