## Where to find SkyTeam award availability

Firstly, as a reminder, these airlines are members of the SkyTeam alliance:

There are two main airline websites that are useful for finding SkyTeam award availability...

## Flying Blue (Air France or KLM) websites

Flying Blue is the frequent flyer program of Air France, KLM and numerous other airlines including Aircalin.

The Air France & KLM websites display award availability on almost all SkyTeam airlines. Both websites have some quirks and takes a little while to get used to, but they are probably the best overall option for SkyTeam searches.

As Air France and KLM use dynamic pricing for their own flights, these reward seats may not be bookable on other Skyteam websites.

One of the great things about the Air France website is that you can see a summary of availability over five full weeks. This makes it easy to find seats on popular routes, if you are flexible with dates. The Delta website has the same feature, but doesn't reliably show award availability on as many of the SkyTeam airlines.

You need a Flying Blue account to search for award seats on the Air France or KLM websites. It's free to join the program.

## Delta Air Lines website

The Delta website shows award availability on many SkyTeam partner airlines, and you don't need a Delta SkyMiles account to search. But there are a few SkyTeam airlines that don't appear on the Delta website, such as Kenya Airways and Czech Airlines. So it's not quite as comprehensive as the Air France or KLM website.

As a quick side note, SkyTeam award availability to/from Australia is generally excellent! As SkyTeam frequent flyer programs are not as accessible to Australians, you'll have very little competition for award seats. Airlines with great availability include Vietnam Airlines and Garuda Indonesia. The most available SkyTeam points currency is Virgin Atlantic Flying Club miles, thanks to its partnership with Amex Membership Rewards.
