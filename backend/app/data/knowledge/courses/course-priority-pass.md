# Priority Pass

**Priority Pass**

Priority Pass membership is handy because you'll have lounge access regardless of the airline you're flying, your status or class of travel. Simply pay per visit or take out an annual membership for unlimited included entries. Various credit cards even come with complimentary Priority Pass membership.

 In this training unit, we will cover...

## Priority Pass lounge network

Airline lounge memberships are best suited to travellers that fly semi-frequently with the same airline.

But Priority Pass membership may be better suited for semi-frequent international travellers that are not loyal to a particular airline or alliance.

You may also be able to get complimentary Priority Pass access through your credit card. Even if you have airline status, this is a great fallback option for times when you may not otherwise have lounge access.

Priority Pass advertises that members have access to over 1,700 airport lounges & restaurants. Around 14 of those airport lounges are in Australia:

Priority Pass currently only partners with a small handful of lounges in domestic airport terminals across Australia. To make up for this, Priority Pass also partners with over 20 airport bars and restaurants in Adelaide, Brisbane, Canberra, Gold Coast, Melbourne, Perth, Sydney and Townsville! In lieu of a lounge visit, **Priority Pass members can get a $36 credit at any of these airport restaurants**.

## Membership options

There are 3 Priority Pass membership levels:

- Standard

- Standard Plus

- Prestige

Here is a summary of the costs and benefits, as per the Priority Pass website:

Priority Pass membership prices & inclusions.

There are no included visits with Standard membership. So if you think you'll visit at least 10 lounges per year, a Standard Plus or Prestige membership would be the best value.

Keep an eye out for discounts sometimes offered on Priority Pass membership. For example, by signing up through Australian Frequent Flyer, you can receive 20% off Standard membership or 10% off Standard Plus membership for the first year.

## Complimentary Priority Pass via credit cards

Rather than purchasing a Priority Pass membership, you could access the same benefits with some credit cards! For example, rather than paying USD469 (~AU$720) for an annual Priority Pass Prestige membership, you could get unlimited Priority Pass visits with an Amex Platinum card, or 15 annual visits with a MyCard Prestige credit card.

## Plaza Premium Lounge Pass

Priority Pass members can access Plaza Premium lounges as part of their membership. But if you just want to use Plaza Premium lounges, you could purchase a Plaza Premium Lounge Pass.

Plaza Premium operates around 100 airport lounges around the world, including several international lounges in Australia & New Zealand. As an alternative to Priority Pass membership, if you're often travelling across the Tasman (or to other international locations where Plaza Premium lounges are available), you could purchase a multi-visit Plaza Premium Lounge Pass at a fixed price.

You can also pre-purchase one-time access to Plaza Premium Lounges on their website.

## Pay-per-use airport lounges

If you don't fly often but would still like to use airport lounges occasionally, pay-per-use lounges could be the solution for you. Many third-party lounges around the world will let you buy one-time entry - either online or at the door - for a nominal fee.

Entry prices vary by lounge, but are normally between around $35 and $80 per visit.

Some airlines will also let you buy one-time lounge access to their lounges if you're flying in Economy and don't have status, although the prices can be quite high. For example, Emirates charges USD155 (~AU$240), excluding tax, for one-time entry to its lounge. Lufthansa offers a similar service at lower prices.

## LoungePair

You may be able to get a lower price by purchasing lounge access using a service such as LoungePair.

LoungePair lets you buy access at a discounted rate to over 1,400+ lounges worldwide without requiring any sort of membership. Prices vary by lounge.

AFF Gold members can even get a $10 discount, and AFF Platinum members can get complimentary LoungePair+ membership for a year! See our LoungePair guide for details.

## Paid lounge access options in Australia

Pay-per-use lounges are generally more common overseas than in Australia. But it is possible to pay for one-time entry to some Australian airport lounges:

- Virgin Australia sells lounge entry at the door for $65 per visit ($45 for accompanying children)

- Rex offers casual lounge entry at the door for $33

- Emirates sells lounge entry for USD155 + tax (or USD125 + tax for Emirates Skywards members)
