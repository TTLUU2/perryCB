# Introduction to Specialised Online Tools

**Introduction to Specialised Online Tools**

Specialised tools designed for finding reward availability can save you time and find options you may otherwise miss.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## The advantages of using specialised search tools

So far we've shown you how to find award availability for free, by using a combination of airline websites. This can be an effective way to find award seats if your search is relatively simple. But there are limitations to using airline websites...

- Most airline websites only show award availability on *some *partner airlines

- Many fail to find 100% of seats that are actually available

- Some show phantom award availability (seats that aren't actually available)

- Searching can be time-consuming, especially on airline websites that only show availability only one day at a time

This is where specialised online tools can come in very handy. Unlike airline websites, **they are specifically designed to make finding award availability easy**. These powerful tools are more thorough, easier to use and will find seats much quicker than you would on your own.

These tools generally *aren't* free. But the subscription price often pays for itself in the form of time savings and the superior results found! These tools often find seats that you may have otherwise overlooked.

## How online tools work

There are many different online tools out there to help with award availability searches.

The original award search tools include:

- KVS Tool

- Award Nexus

- ExpertFlyer

In addition to these, there are now an increasing number of next-generation award search tools that are a bit more user-friendly and make it even easier to find award availability. These include:

- seats.aero

- SeatSpy

- AwardLogic

- Point.me

- Gyoza Flights

- Reward Flight Finder

- AwardFares

- PointsYeah

- Roame

- AwardTool

- SeatFinder

- FlightSeats.io

- Points Ninja

- Virgin Atlantic’s Reward seat checker

- Awardhacks.se (for SAS availability)

We'll cover several of these tools in detail later in this unit.

**Many of these tools simply automate searches that you could do yourself for free on the various airline websites that we looked at in the previous module.** In fact, the KVS Tool and Award Nexus literally log into airline websites, searching on your behalf and scraping the results.

But the benefit of using these tools is that they can **search much more quickly**. Rather than searching for one date at a time, some tools can even present you with an overview of all available reward seats on a given route on a single page. They may also find options you may have missed when searching yourself, and some allow you to set reward seat alerts.

While none of these tools are 100% perfect, they can certainly make your life easier.

## The different types of online award search tools

While there are many different online tools that can help you with finding reward availability, some of them are quite similar to each other.

Here are the five main types of tools that you will come across...

## 1. Legacy tools

KVS Tool and Award Nexus are two of the original award search tools. These basically just automate airline website searches that you could do yourself.

These tools are both still supported, but to be honest, there are now much better options.

## KVS Tool

In particular, KVS Tool is rather clunky and requires you to download software onto your computer. This software is not compatible with Apple computers, requiring the use of additional third-party CrossOver software, and you need to enter your frequent flyer account details to be able to do many searches.

You can download KVS Tool for free but need a paid subscription to use much of the functionality.

KVS Tool search results look something like this:

Results of KVS Tool search for Star Alliance award availability from LAX to FRA.

Under "Award Availability", available seats on each flight are represented by three characters. The first letter of each entry refers to the class of travel:

- F = First class

- C = Business class

- W = Premium economy

- Y = Economy

The second letter refers to the award "type". This is generally "S", referring to Standard award availability but may also be M, A, L, H, R, E or D. Each character denotes a specific award type, unique to that airline.

The third character refers to the number of available seats for that particular award type and class:

- “+” = at least the number of seats requested are available

- “-” = the requested number of seats are not available

- “@” = the requested number of seats are not available but waitlist is open

- “/” = cabin not applicable

- “~” = information could not be found

- Any numeric value means that at least that number of seats are available.

## Award Nexus

Award Nexus works in a similar way to the KVS tool, by screen scraping the results of airline website searches and presenting the information in a way that is easy to read. But it's a lot more user-friendly, being a website (not a downloadable tool) and it doesn't require you to provide frequent flyer program membership details.

Award Nexus works on a "points" system. Basically, it costs 1 point for every airline website, date and class of travel that you include in each search. See the Award Nexus website for more information.

## 2. Tools that pull information directly from the GDS

## ExpertFlyer

ExpertFlyer works a little bit differently, getting most of its data straight from the GDS (Global Distribution System, which is used by travel agents). As it doesn't rely on airline websites, this means ExpertFlyer can also show award availability for more niche airlines that aren't part of any alliance and don't have many partner airlines.

We'll cover ExpertFlyer in detail towards the end of this course.

## 3. Tools that search for lots of options on a specific route & date

Tools such as **Point.me**, **AwardLogic**, **PointsYeah** and **Roame** are useful if you want to find reward seat availability on a specific route and travel date.

Although you can only search for one route and date at a time, these tools search for available reward seats on a wide range of different airlines. This is most helpful of your travel plans are fixed but you're flexible with airlines and your routing. It's also helpful if you have points with a credit card rewards program that you can transfer to multiple different frequent flyer programs.

We'll cover two of these tools in detail - Point.me and AwardLogic - in the next training unit.

## 4. Tools that show availability on a specific route & airline across a whole month or year

There are a range of tools that only let you search for reward availability on one specific airline & route at a time, but can instantly show you all of the available reward seats for that route across an entire month or year!

This is most helpful if your travel dates are flexible, but you want to fly on a particular route and cabin class.

SeatSpy is perhaps the most useful of these tools, and we'll cover it later in this course. But there are a few other tools that work on specific airlines:

- Reward Flight Finder can show you an entire year's worth of award availability on any British Airways route

- Virgin Atlantic's free Reward seat checker tool can show you all the reward availability on any Virgin Atlantic or Delta route to/from the UK in a given month

Example of a Reward Flight Finder search for British Airways availability between London and Los Angeles.

## 5. Tools with multiple talents

As an all-round reward flight searching tool, it's hard to beat **Seats.aero**. This excellent tool has many of the features shown above, all in one place. For Australians, it even covers Qantas Frequent Flyer and Velocity Frequent Flyer.

We'll cover Seats.aero in detail towards the end of this course.

AwardFares also offers a similar service that can help with finding Oneworld, Star Alliance or SkyTeam award availability. Features include an award search tool, calendar, award map and availability alerts.
