# Online Upgrade Auctions

**Online Upgrade Auctions**

As an alternative to upgrading with frequent flyer points, many airlines offer the opportunity to bid for an upgrade. This method is great for securing a cheap upgrade.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## How online upgrade auctions work

Many airlines now offer the chance to bid for an upgrade on your flight. This can be a very cost-effective way to upgrade, with minimum bids typically much lower than the fare difference between Economy and Business class.

Online upgrade auctions have become more and more popular in recent years. Airlines love them as they can use them to fill otherwise empty premium cabins. And it's good for flyers too, as it offers another way to upgrade for a very low price. In fact, you can choose your own price!

Here's how it works...

## 1. Passengers are invited to place bids

Some airlines, such as Virgin Australia and Air New Zealand, offer all passengers the opportunity to bid for an upgrade from the time of booking. If this is the case, you can make an offer while managing your booking online.

Other airlines, including Qantas and Etihad Airways, may only invite a few selected passengers to bid. In this case, invitations are usually only sent if expected sales in the premium cabin are low. Auctions may open 1 to 2 weeks before the flight, or many months in advance.

Bidding usually closes around 2-3 days before the flight, but it depends on the airline.

## 2. Choosing a bid amount

The airline will set a minimum and maximum bid amount, and it’s up to you to choose any amount within this range. To give you a basic idea, the minimum bid for a shorter domestic flight might be around $100. For a trans-Tasman flight, you’d usually be looking at around $100-200. Minimum bid amounts do vary, even for flights on the same route.

You won't know how many other people have placed a bid, nor how much they've offered. So there is a little bit of guesswork and luck involved.

## 3. Receiving the outcome

After placing a bid, you'll be notified of the outcome a few days before the flight departs. If you were successful, your credit card will be charged for the offer amount. If your bid wasn't successful, no money will be charged. So you've really got nothing to lose by placing a bid!

Email from Air New Zealand confirming a successful upgrade bid.

If you're upgraded, you'll only earn points and status credits for the fare class originally booked.

Most airlines use the Plusgrade platform to process upgrade bids.

## Qantas Bid Now upgrades

Example of a Qantas Bid Now upgrade offer.

Qantas "Bid Now" upgrades are offered on an invitation-only basis. If Qantas decides to invite you to place a bid, you'll get an email around 1-2 weeks before the flight. Bids close 24 hours before take-off and you'll be notified of the outcome shortly thereafter.

The Qantas system lets you bid a mixture of Qantas points and money. The minimum cash bid can be reduced by bidding a greater number of points.

When comparing bids, we believe that Qantas converts any points you've bid to money. This is probably done at a rate of around 0.6-0.8 cents per point. That isn’t great value, so we would suggest only bidding money. If you want to place a bid above the minimum amount, add money to the bid.

Bid Now upgrades could be useful if you're short on points, or if you're booked in a fare class that is not upgradable using Qantas points. But if you have enough points in your account, we would generally suggest requesting a Classic Upgrade Reward instead. Qantas point upgrades not only receive priority over Bid Now, but they are also generally better value.

## Virgin Australia UpgradeMe Premium Bids

An example of a minimum Virgin Australia upgrade bid.

Virgin Australia offers what they call an "UpgradeMe Premium Bid". All fare types except Reward Seat bookings & Economy Lite fares are eligible to place a bid. You'll be able to do so on the Virgin Australia website immediately after booking the flight. Bids close 4 hours before departure, and you'll be informed of the outcome up to 24 hours before the flight.

Bids on Virgin Australia can only be made using money, and not Velocity points.

In practice, Virgin does often accept the minimum bid and the rates are usually reasonable. So this is a great way to upgrade on Virgin Australia without spending a fortune!

## Air New Zealand OneUp upgrades

Example of an Air New Zealand OneUp upgrade offer.

Air New Zealand has a similar system called "OneUp". This is one of the only ways to score a Business class seat on Air New Zealand without buying it outright. (Air New Zealand very rarely releases any award availability!) To upgrade an Air New Zealand flight, you can place a bid any time after booking. Successful bids can be paid for using money, Airpoints or any combination of the two.

Bids made by Airpoints elite members are "up-weighted" by 10-50%, depending on the member's status level.

## Tricks for a successful bid

## 1. Check the flight loading

Before placing a bid, conduct a Flight Availability search using ExpertFlyer. (See the first unit of this course to learn more about interpreting fare class information on ExpertFlyer.)

By checking the flight's loading, you'll get an approximate idea of how many seats remain unsold in the premium cabin. If there are many vacant seats, there is a good chance you'll be successful with a low bid. There's no need to bid a high amount if there are still lots of free seats!

But if there aren't too many seats left, you may need to place a bid that's a bit more competitive. After all, priority goes to the highest bidders.

Swiss Airbus A340-300 Business Class. Photo: Matt Graham.

## 2. Bid at the last minute

Don't bid many months before the flight! Most airlines allow bids to be made up until a few days or even hours before the flight. If you wait until closer to the flight, you'll have access to a much more accurate picture of the flight loads. You can then plan your bid strategically, according to this.

If you're planning to place a bid, why not set a reminder on your phone to make an offer a couple of days before the cut-off time?

## 3. Bid (slightly) more than the minimum amount

Bid at least *slightly *more than the minimum. Most people are likely to bid the minimum amount and hope for the best. If you bid just a little bit more than the minimum, you're instantly giving yourself a better chance of success for little extra cost.

Many people cheekily bid the minimum amount. So, even bidding $5 more than this can give you an advantage. Photo: Adobe Stock.

## 4. Travel solo

You're more likely to get the upgrade if you're travelling alone. This applies to any type of upgrade, not just auctions. Travelling solo is optimal, because it's far easier for an airline to upgrade a single passenger than an entire family. For example, there may be two Business seats available. If you were travelling as a group of three, you would miss out.

## 5. Seek out leisure routes

You're more likely to get an upgrade travelling to leisure destinations. The simple reason is that the premium cabin will be filled with fewer paying business travellers. This leaves more seats free for anyone wanting a cheap upgrade!

Travelling at times that business travellers don't typically travel can also help. This could mean travelling during the middle of the week, or on Saturday nights/Sunday mornings (on short-haul routes).
