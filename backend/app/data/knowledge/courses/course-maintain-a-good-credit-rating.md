# Maintain a Good Credit Rating

**Maintain a Good Credit Rating**

A good credit rating is essential when applying for credit cards (among other things). In this unit, learn how to maintain a good credit rating and how to check your current standing.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## What are credit ratings?

A credit rating is a **measurement of a customer's perceived risk to a potential credit provider**. A high credit rating indicates a proven track record of repaying debts on time.

There are a few different types of credit rating models. With the Equifax model, for example, your credit rating will be a number between 0 and 1,200. According to GetCreditScore, an Equifax credit rating can be considered on the following scale:

The ranges of Equifax credit ratings in the below average, average, good, very good and excellent categories.

## Why are credit ratings important?

Your credit rating is a very important thing! The higher your credit rating, the more likely you are to get a credit application approved.

In terms of earning points, a good credit rating gives you access to more credit cards which you can use to earn hundreds of thousands of points.

A good credit rating also helps you to access other credit products including loans, which could be extremely important if you want to buy a house!

Conversely, a poor credit rating means you'll likely be rejected when you apply for a new card, loan or any other type of credit.

When applying for credit cards, it is vital that you have - and maintain - a good credit score. Every credit provider will check this before approving your application.

## How to maintain a good credit rating

## 1. Don't apply for too many cards within a short period of time

Every time you apply for a new card, your credit rating will take a small hit – regardless of whether you are approved. This is not a major concern if you apply for cards over a long period of time. But avoid applying for too many cards within a short time because it may look like you are struggling financially. If the bank believes this to be the case, it's unlikely they will approve your application for credit. As a general rule, try not to apply for more than one or two cards every three months.

## 2. **Always **pay off your card on-time and in-full each month

This is extremely important. Do not get yourself into credit card debt! This is a sure-fire way to damage your credit rating, and you will risk having future applications declined. You'll also end up paying a lot of money in interest payments. This may be common-sense financial advice, but never make purchases on your credit card that you are not able to repay.

Remember, credit cards are most useful to frequent flyers as a means of earning points and enjoying the associated perks. If you're using credit cards primarily as a credit facility, you probably need to reconsider having them.

## 3. Maintain a low "credit to debt" ratio

Try to keep the amount owing on your card/s well below your credit limit.

## 4. Keep your cards "open" as long as possible

Finally, try not to churn through credit cards *too *quickly. Credit card churning is the practice of applying for cards, getting the bonus points and then cancelling the card. Keeping a card open for a long period of time, while paying it off on-time each month, will slowly improve your credit score over time. So, unless there's a good reason to cancel a particular card, it will often improve your credit score just to keep it open.

Having said that, there are legitimate reasons to close a card. If you're not using the card and a large annual fee is due, this could be a good reason to cancel it. You may also choose to cancel the card to reset the period in which you'll qualify as a new customer (in order to get bonus points again from the same bank).

The main thing is to be responsible. Credit card "churning" works only as a long-term strategy. **Do not get too carried away and apply for multiple cards at once!**

## How to check your own credit report

Before applying for new credit cards, you may wish to check your current credit rating. This can be easily done online for free!

Multiple websites offer free credit checks, including GetCreditScore. This website will let you know your credit rating for free, and the process only takes around a minute.

Example of a Get Credit Score rating.

For a more detailed credit check, Equifax offers one free credit report once every three months. They also offer paid subscriptions that come with access to faster information and more services.

The advantage of checking your credit report - rather than just your rating - is that it gives you the opportunity to identity any errors. If you find a mistake in your credit report that is affecting your credit rating, you might be able to have it corrected.
