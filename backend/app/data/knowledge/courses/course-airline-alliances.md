# Airline Alliances

**Airline Alliances**

If you want to fly somewhere in Australia, Qantas or Virgin Australia can probably get you there. But no airline flies to every single destination in the world. This is where the three global airline alliances - Oneworld, Star Alliance and SkyTeam - come in handy.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## What are airline alliances?

An alliance is a partnership of airlines from around the world. Airlines in an alliance typically cooperate in a range of areas, such as by increasing connectivity and providing reciprocal benefits to frequent flyers of any other airline in the alliance. From a customer's perspective, they create a more seamless travel experience when travelling to a part of the world not covered by their home airline.

You can earn frequent flyer points and status credits when travelling on any airline within your home airline's alliance. You can also enjoy the benefits of your status and redeem points with any other airline in the alliance. So, for example, you can earn Qantas points when flying with American Airlines because both Qantas and American Airlines are part of the Oneworld alliance.

Let's take a closer look at the three major alliances…

## Oneworld

The **one**world alliance is the most widely-used by Australian flyers because Qantas is a member. The Oneworld alliance covers Australia well, although there are a few network gaps. There are currently no member airlines from mainland China, and Oneworld has limited coverage in South America, Africa and central Europe.

When you earn status with any Oneworld airline, you will automatically earn an equivalent "Oneworld status" level. Oneworld status tiers are:

- Oneworld **Ruby** (equivalent to Qantas Silver)

- Oneworld **Sapphire** (equivalent to Qantas Gold)

- Oneworld **Emerald** (equivalent to Qantas Platinum or Platinum One)

## Star Alliance

Star Alliance has the most member airlines of any alliance. Star Alliance has good coverage in most parts of the world, especially Europe. There are no Star Alliance airlines based in Australia, but many do fly here.

There are two Star Alliance status tiers:

- Star Alliance **Silver**

- Star Alliance **Gold**

Unlike Oneworld, Star Alliance does not have a top-tier status level that is equivalent to most airlines' Platinum tier. This means that with Star Alliance, the extra benefits of top-tier status (where offered) are only available on the airline with which the status is earned.

In August 2022, Deutsche Bahn (a German railway company) joined Star Alliance as an "intermodal partner". Note that ITA Airways, based in Italy, will join Star Alliance in 2026.

## SkyTeam

SkyTeam is the third major global alliance. SkyTeam has a strong presence in Asia, particularly in China, and a number of SkyTeam airlines fly to Australia. But it lacks a strong presence in Australia, and for this reason it is often overlooked by Australian flyers.

There are two SkyTeam status tiers:

- Elite (equivalent to Silver with most airlines)

- Elite Plus (equivalent to Gold with most airlines)

As with Star Alliance, there is no SkyTeam status level above Elite Plus.

## Airline partnerships outside of alliances

Most major international airlines are members of one of the three global airline alliances. But many smaller airlines - and even some large ones - are *not* members.

These airlines often create virtual networks of their own partner airlines. For example, Virgin Australia is not part of any alliance. But it has a selection of its own specially chosen partners including Singapore Airlines, Qatar Airways and United Airlines.

Even most airlines that *are* part of an alliance create their own unique partnerships with other airlines. For example, Qantas has extensive partnerships with Emirates (not in an alliance) and China Eastern (a SkyTeam member).

These partnerships create some very interesting opportunities when it comes to earning and burning points!

## Common mistakes

Beware of these common misconceptions with airline partnerships...

**1. It is not possible to transfer points to another frequent flyer program**

You can redeem your points to fly with other airlines, but you can't *transfer* them to another airline's frequent flyer program. There are exceptions to this rule, but they are rare.

**2. It is not generally possible to redeem points for upgrades on partner airlines**

Generally speaking, you can only use your points to upgrade on the airline with which you earned the points. There are some exceptions like Star Alliance Upgrade Awards, which we'll discuss in Course 7 (Everybody Loves an Upgrade).
