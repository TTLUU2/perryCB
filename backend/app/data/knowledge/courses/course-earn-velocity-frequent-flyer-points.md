## Introduction to Velocity Frequent Flyer

Velocity Frequent Flyer is the loyalty program of Virgin Australia. It is the major competitor to Qantas Frequent Flyer and Australia's second-largest frequent flyer program.

Virgin Australia has gone to much effort to make its Velocity Frequent Flyer program a serious competitor to Qantas since its launch in 2011. Key benefits of Velocity over Qantas Frequent Flyer include:

- Fewer points required for most reward flights

- Fewer status credits required to earn & maintain elite status

- Family pooling

- Complimentary Business Class upgrade vouchers for Platinum members

- No joining fee

- "Fly Ahead" for Velocity Gold & Platinum members (on eligible fare types)

- Ability to transfer Velocity points to Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer miles

But there are also some disadvantages:

- Virgin Australia flies to very few international destinations

- Fewer international partner airlines and Virgin Australia not part of an alliance

- Virgin Australia has airport lounges at fewer Australian airports, no dedicated Business Lounges and none of its own international lounges

Velocity is introducing changes to its frequent flyer program in 2025, including the launch of "Forever Gold" lifetime status tier, and a Platinum Plus tier above Platinum.

## Key facts

- **Airline:** Virgin Australia

- **Currency:** Velocity points

- **Status** is earned by accumulating the required status credits in any 12-month period

- **Status levels:** Red, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Platinum Plus (coming October 2025)

- **Points expire** if there is no account activity for 2 years

- Up to four family transfers of Velocity points are permitted per year, with up to 100,000 points allowed to be transferred at a time. Velocity also offers family pooling.

- **Our program rating:** 4/5

## Earning points on Virgin Australia flights

On Virgin Australia domestic and international short-haul flights, you'll earn 4 Velocity points for every $1 spent on the airfare. If you have earned points with an eligible Velocity Frequent Flyer co-branded credit card in the past 12 months, this increases to 5 points per dollar.

Velocity Silver, Gold and Platinum members also earn 50%, 75% and 100% bonus points respectively, except on Economy Lite airfares.

See the Velocity Frequent Flyer website for details.

## Earning points on partner airlines

This is where the Velocity Frequent Flyer program can get very confusing. The earn rate with partner airlines is based on the number of miles flown. But the exact rate at which points are earned will depend on the airline and fare class.

The rates are different for each of Virgin Australia's partner airlines. Some fare classes may not be eligible to earn any Velocity Frequent Flyer points.

See the Velocity Frequent Flyer website for details of earn rates with each partner airline.

## Earning Velocity points on the ground

There are many ways to earn Velocity points on the ground in Australia...

## Credit cards

Earning Velocity points from credit cards is very easy. Most major banks offer co-branded Velocity credit cards, and Velocity is also a transfer partner of almost every Australia credit card rewards program.

If you're considering transferring your credit card points to Velocity, keep an eye out for the 15% bonus point promotions. These typically occur twice-yearly in May and November.

Credit cards are covered in detail in the next course.

## Shopping

Velocity's has a wide range of retail partners in Australia including 7-Eleven and Myer, and you can earn points with DoorDash. See the full list of earning partners on the Velocity website.

Over 250 online retailers participate in the Velocity eStore. These include Apple, Appliances Online, BCF, The Body Shop and David Jones. See the Velocity eStore website for more details.

You can also transfer Flybuys points to Velocity. Flybuys earning partners include Coles, K-Mart and Target. Flybuys is covered in detail in Course 9.

You can earn Velocity points at 7-Eleven petrol stations and convenience stores. Photo: 7-Eleven.

## Booking Hotels & Car Hire

Velocity points can be earned by hiring cars from Europcar, Thrifty, Hertz, Dollar and SIXT.

You can also earn Velocity points for staying at a Velocity partner hotel or by booking hotels through Virgin Australia Hotels (powered by Hopper) or Agoda PointsMax.

## DiDi

Earn 1 Velocity point per dollar on the base fare for every DiDi ride you take within Australia. You just need to link your DiDi and Velocity accounts in the DiDi app.

You can earn Velocity Frequent Flyer Points with DiDi. Photo: Virgin Australia.

## E-Rewards

Velocity points can be earned for free by completing online surveys with e-Rewards. You'll earn Opinion Points for completing surveys which can later be transferred to Velocity. Participation in E-Rewards is free but the surveys can be time-consuming.
