# Effortlessly Manage Travel Bookings

**Effortlessly Manage Travel Bookings**

If you travel regularly, it can become difficult to keep track every flight, hotel and other travel booking. Luckily, there are some free tools that do all the hard work for you!

In this training unit, we will cover…

## TripIt

If you travel a lot, it can be hard to keep track of all those bookings. This is where a service such as TripIt becomes invaluable. It's a bit like having a personal assistant.

TripIt is relatively easy to use. You can set it up so that all of your travel booking confirmation emails are automatically forwarded to TripIt. This includes flight, hotel, train, bus, car hire and many other types of tickets and bookings. You can also manually forward or manually add bookings in TripIt.

TripIt then collates all of your bookings and creates a master itinerary. But it doesn't just organise information, it adds value as well. For example, TripIt can tell you the weather forecast for your destination and provide directions to your hotel. TripIt will also alert you if one of your flights changes or is delayed.

You can access your bookings from anywhere on the TripIt website or via the TripIt smartphone App. You can also easily share your itinerary with family or colleagues.

The basic features of TripIt are completely free to use. You can access additional features such as seat alerts and refund notifications by subscribing to TripIt Pro for an annual fee. In our view, the free version is perfectly adequate and many of the pro features are covered by ExpertFlyer and AwardWallet. But it could be worth subscribing to TripIt Pro if you really travel a lot.

TripIt in action.

## TripCase

TripIt doesn't have a monopoly in this space. TripCase is another service that manages your travel bookings for you, and most features are free.

TripCase is arguably easier to use than TripIt, but there's one key disadvantage. Unlike TripIt, emails that you receive will not be automatically forwarded to TripCase unless the booking is made with a TripCase affiliate. But it is easy enough to manually forward your emails to TripCase.

TripCase in action.

## Flighty

If you're a frequent flyer, it's handy to be across any flight delays before they happen.

The Flighty App is one of the best tools out there for tracking your flights and getting notified about potential delays. It's one of the more expensive options, but it's worth it.

This App was developed by self-described "flight nerds" who have put a lot of effort into making sure the App's notifications are timely and accurate. It gives you a lot of data, right at your fingertips, but it's also very easy to use.

One cool feature is that it also keeps an archive of your past flights. Flighty even displays the *actual* flight path that you took, rather than just the most direct route between the two airports.

Screenshot from the Flighty App.

## Other flight tracking apps

Flight Aware is another website that probably knows if your flight will be delayed before the airline does! The website tracks all commercial flights in real time, and is usually accurate. The “track inbound plane” feature is especially handy.

Flightradar24 is also useful for real-time flight tracking. You can also use Flightradar24 to track the previous flights flown by any aircraft.

## Useful airline websites

Sometimes, there are certain things that you want to do but that your own airline's website won't let you. This is where a few specific airline websites can come in very handy!

## Royal Jordanian website (**one**world)

Want to double-check that your Qantas booking has been ticketed correctly? By plugging your Qantas booking reference (PNR) into the "Manage Booking" portal on rj.com, you can view the current ticket number associated with your reservation:

An example of a "Manage Booking" page on the Royal Jordanian Airlines website.

The Royal Jordanian website may also let you select seats on Qantas and other Oneworld or Amadeus bookings where you haven't been able to select seats on the operating airline's website.

## Finnair website (**one**world)

Want to get a copy of your ticket with an itemised breakdown of all of the fare components, taxes & charges? If you have an Amadeus PNR, enter this into the Manage Booking flow on the Finnair website.

When your booking comes up, go to "Passenger details". You should then be able to see the e-ticket number for the booking, the name and frequent flyer numbers of any passengers, and an option to get an e-ticket receipt. This receipt will be emailed to you and includes a detailed breakdown of the fare paid. This also works for frequent flyer reward bookings.

Download an itemised e-ticket receipt from the Finnair website.

## Czech Airlines website (SkyTeam)

The Czech Airlines website will often let you select seats on flights operated by some SkyTeam partner airlines, even when the operating airline's website won't let you.

Plug your booking reference into the Czech Airlines website, then scroll down to the "Additional services" option.

You'll then be able to enter the seat selection page:

You can use the Czech Airlines website to select seats on other partner airlines.

## Saudia website (SkyTeam)

The Saudia website can be useful if you want to check the ticket number, status, fare breakdown or fare class on a SkyTeam booking.

You can bring up your booking information by plugging your SkyTeam PNR into Saudia's website. Then, click on "View" beside where it says "E-ticket" to access detailed information about your booking status.

View detailed information about your booking on the Saudia website.
