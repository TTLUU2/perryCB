# Alaska Airlines Atmos Rewards

**Alaska Airlines Atmos Rewards**

The Atmos Rewards program of Alaska Airlines (and now also Hawaiian Airlines) offers good value redemptions with Oneworld and a range of niche partner airlines.

In this training unit, we will cover...

## Why buy Atmos Rewards points?

The Alaska Airlines Atmos Rewards program (previously called "Mileage Plan") offers good value on redemptions with its Oneworld alliance partners and over 10 other niche partner airlines. Short-haul redemptions including many Qantas trans-Tasman flights are particularly good value. Plus, there are no fuel surcharges when flying on most partner airlines!

Alaska Airlines is part of the Oneworld alliance. Photo: Alaska Airlines.

## Buying Atmos Rewards points

Alaska Airlines Atmos Rewards points cost USD37.63 per thousand (including the 7.5% US Federal Excise tax).

The program typically offers between around 40% and 60% bonus points every 2-3 months. These are not as generous as some of the other US-based programs. **But**, Atmos Rewards points may be more valuable.

Promotions may be targeted. This means you'll need to be a member to receive an offer, and also that different members may receive different offers at the same time. If you're a new member, the first transaction will usually take up to 24 hours to process.

Since 2024, beware that Alaska Airlines has been geo-blocking some of its bonus point offers.

There is a purchase limit of 100,000 points per transaction. You can make multiple transactions, however there is also an annual purchase limit of 150,000 points (including bonus points). This annual purchase limit is waived if you have Atmos Rewards status.

You can purchase points on the Alaska Airlines website.

## Award charts

Previously, you could only redeem Alaskan miles on specific routes. This restriction is now gone and you can use Atmos Rewards points to fly on any routes with award availability.

In April 2024, new award charts came into effect that take into account the regions you're flying between and the total distance travelled. For example, this is the award chart for flights to/from the Asia-Pacific region (including Australia):

Beware that these are "starting at" prices, and may not be available on all partner airlines.

See the Alaska Airlines website for award flight pricing on other routes. For redemptions including Alaska Airlines flights, dynamic pricing applies. Alaska also uses dynamic award pricing on some of its partner airlines.

## How to book

Atmos Rewards redemptions are available for all partner airlines on the Alaska Airlines website.

Simply check the "Use points" box when searching for one-way or return flights. Alternatively, click on "All search options" to make a multi-city booking or add a stopover.

Book award flights on the Alaska Airlines website. Click "Use points" to search for award seats. For multi-city flights, including adding stopovers, select "All search options".

If you are unable to book online, you can call Alaska Airlines on **+1 (800) 252 7522**.

## Award sweet spots

Qantas domestic and short-haul international flights under 1,500 miles can be great value at just 7,500 Atmos Rewards points in Economy or 15,000 points in Business (plus taxes).

For example, you could fly Qantas Business Class from Australia to New Zealand for just 15,000 points + taxes.

Redeeming Atmos Rewards points for Qantas trans-Tasman flights can be good value. Screenshot from the Alaska Airlines website.

You can also use 15,000 points for Qatar Airways regional First Class. This is the same physical seat as you'd get in Business Class, but a ticket will get you access to the Al Safwa First Class lounge in Doha! In general, the "under 1,500 mile" sweet spot is a great use of points, in both Economy and Business Class.

Awards between 7,000 and 10,000 miles in length such as Melbourne-Doha-Vienna are also competitively priced at 85,000 points one-way.

## Award routing rules

- One-way redemptions are allowed, and cost no extra compared to booking return flights

- One stopover is allowed on most one-way international award bookings, although it generally must be in a city you would have to connect through anyway

- Premium Economy awards are not available with all partners

- Icelandair Saga Class prices as Premium Economy

Flying Cathay Pacific or Japan Airlines with Atmos Rewards points can be great value, if you can find award availability. Photo: Matt Graham.

## Fees on Atmos Rewards award bookings

The following fees apply for Atmos Rewards award bookings:

- Partner airline booking fee: USD12.50 (charged per direction)

- Phone booking fee: USD15 (this is payable even for bookings that are not possible online)

Since May 2021, there are no longer fees for changing or cancelling an award booking.

## Summary: Buying Atmos Rewards points

- Points cost USD37.63 per thousand

- Promotions are frequent and a typical offer is 100% bonus points, but these may be targeted to US residents

- Points can be redeemed on Oneworld and various niche partner airlines

- Short-haul awards under 1,500 miles are particularly good value, but pricing is generally reasonable on most routes
