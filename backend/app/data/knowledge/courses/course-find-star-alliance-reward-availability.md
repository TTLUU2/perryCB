## Where to find Star Alliance reward availability

There are three Star Alliance airline websites that show reward availability across most or all Star Alliance carriers. Each website has its own quirks, but all are relatively easy to use. There's also a useful third-party website that shows award space for Star Alliance.

Star Alliance member airlines.

## United Airlines website

The United website is by far the most user-friendly of the three websites. You don't need an account to search - simply tick "search for award travel" when searching for flights from the United home page.

As well as being easy to use, the United website has a 30-day calendar that allows you to see availability across a whole month simultaneously. You can also narrow down your search to only show non-stop flights.

When viewing availability on United's own flights, make sure you search for Saver Awards, and not "Everyday" Awards (denoted by a fare class with two letters, such as "JN"). Only Saver Awards (with a fare class such as X or I) are available to book using partner frequent flyer programs.

## Air Canada website

The Air Canada website also displays award availability for all Star Alliance airlines. As there are very few award routing rules with the Aeroplan program, Air Canada may also show more obscure routings that other websites miss. However, there is no longer an award availability calendar so you'll need to search one day at a time.

Screenshot from the Air Canada website.

Unfortunately, the Air Canada results are not *always* 100% reliable. The website also has an annoying habit of putting Air Canada flights at the top of search results, even if there are more direct options on other airlines and the Air Canada flights shown are not available to members of partner airline programs.

Air Canada may also show options on some airlines that are not part of Star Alliance, such as Etihad Airways or Virgin Australia. If you're searching for long-haul award seats to/from Australia, this could actually be useful because you might find a Star Alliance flight option out of another Australian city with a Virgin Australia domestic connection. (Wouldn't you like to know if you're searching for a flight from Sydney to Los Angeles and there's an available flight out of Melbourne?)

You do not need to be an Aeroplan member to search for award availability on the Air Canada website. However, it is easier to search if you are logged in.

## All Nippon Airways (ANA) website

Like the United website, this is a user-friendly option for Star Alliance award availability searches.

The main downsides of the ANA website are that you'll need to create an account before you can search, and you'll only be able to search for seats one day at a time.

Again, beware that not all ANA-operated flights shown in the results will be available to book via partner airline frequent flyer programs.
