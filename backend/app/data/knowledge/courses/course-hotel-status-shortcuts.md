# Hotel Status Shortcuts

**Hotel Status Shortcuts**

Just like the airlines, many hotels offer simple status matches or challenges. You may also be able to attain hotel elite status through your credit card or airline status.

In this training unit, we will cover…

## Mattress runs

If you're just a little bit short of obtaining a higher status tier with your preferred hotel loyalty program, you might consider doing a "mattress run".

**Mattress runs are the hotel equivalent of airline status runs.** A mattress run is where someone checks into a hotel for the purpose of accumulating annual nights with their hotel loyalty program. These nights count towards attaining a higher hotel status.

Hotel loyalty programs award status based on how much you stay with that hotel chain each year. But some programs have different requirements based on "nights" and "stays". For example, Hilton awards top-tier Diamond status if you stay either 60 nights or 30 times during the year. An easy way to exploit this loophole is to switch hotels each night, racking up "stays" instead of "nights".

Mattress runs are the hotel equivalent of airline status runs. Photo: Matt Graham.

Some people may even check into a cheap hotel and then leave. This doesn't seem logical, but it could be worth it if you're one night short of the next tier and have no further travel planned that year.

Note that most hotel loyalty programs will consider it a single "stay" if you check out and back into the same hotel on the same day.

## Hotel status matches

Mattress runs can help if you want to earn hotel status the traditional way. But there are lots of other ways to earn status without doing the hard yards!

For example, if you already have status with at least one hotel chain, you may be able to leverage this for a status match with another one.

Many hotel loyalty programs are very liberal when it comes to status matches. By simply asking, many programs will be happy to match your status, based on your current level with another hotel program.

There are also several specific promotions available...

## Best Western Status Match

It's not the most exciting hotel chain, but one program that offers very easy status matches is Best Western Rewards. This program is so keen to give out status matches, in fact, that it advertises them on the home page of their website.

All you'll need to do to get a status match with Best Western, is to fill out a short online form. As long as you already hold status with another program, your application will almost certainly be approved. It's really that easy!

## Other hotel status matches

The following hotel loyalty programs are also currently offering status matches:

- Choice Privileges (Australia, NZ, USA, Canada & Europe residents)

- Wyndham Rewards

Even if your desired hotel chain is not currently running a status match promotion, it still doesn't hurt to ask. If you can provide a solid case as to why a hotel should give you a status match, then why not send them an email or give them a call?

You can read about the recent experiences of others who've requested hotel status matches on the StatusMatcher website.

## Hotel status challenges

Numerous hotel loyalty programs also offer status *challenges*. These work in a similar way to the status challenges offered by some airlines. To earn status through a challenge, you'll be tasked with reaching certain target with a short period of time. This generally involves a minimum number of stays or nights within a 3-month period.

## Hilton Honors Status Match Challenge

Hilton Honors offers a Status Match Challenge. Here are the challenge targets:

- **Gold challenge** - stay 8 nights at Hilton hotels within 90 days

- **Diamond challenge** - stay 14 nights at Hilton hotels within 90 days

To receive a Gold or Diamond challenge, you'll need to already hold an equivalent status level in another program. You will receive the benefits of your Hilton status during the initial 90 days. If you complete the minimum nights within 90 days, you'll keep your status until 31 March 2025.

See the Hilton website for more information.

Photo: Hilton.

## Other hotel status challenge promotions

The following hotel programs are also currently offering status challenges:

- **Marriott Bonvoy** – Marriott offers a Platinum Elite Status Challenge that requires you to stay 16 (paid) nights at Marriott-affiliated hotels within 3 months. Alternatively, their Gold challenge requires you to stay 8 (paid) nights within 3 months. Contact Marriott Bonvoy to request a challenge.

## Hotel status fast-track for Velocity members

If you hold status with Virgin Australia's Velocity program, there's another hotel status fast-track you can use.

Velocity Gold and Platinum members receive an instant status match to an equivalent status level with their choice of either Hilton Honors or IHG One Rewards:

If you're eligible, simply request the status match by logging into your Velocity Frequent Flyer account.

This status match is generally valid until the end of the following year. After that, you would need to meet the usual criteria to retain that status level.

In the case of Hilton, there are no soft landings. So even if you have Hilton Honors Diamond status, you could drop back down to Hilton Blue membership once it expires.

Unfortunately, you can only get this status match once per Velocity tier during your lifetime.

## Other airline fast-tracks

Various other frequent flyer programs also offer hotel status fast-tracks to their elite frequent flyer members:

- **Qantas Frequent Flyer** Gold and Points Club Plus members can get a fast track to **Accor ALL** Silver status by linking accounts and completing one stay with Accor. Qantas Points Club members need to complete at least two stays, each of at least two nights, to get the same match.

- **United MileagePlus** offers an instant **Marriott Bonvoy** status match to frequent flyers with Premier Gold or higher status.

- **American Airlines AAdvantage** also has a partnership with **World of Hyatt**. AAdvantage members may be targeted with status match offers for Hyatt, and vice versa for World of Hyatt members. These offers are typically sent via email.

## Earning hotel status via credit cards

The Australian **American Express Platinum** charge card comes with complimentary hotel status. The annual fee is very high at $1,450. But among other card benefits, you'll receive:

- Hilton Honors Gold status

- Marriott Bonvoy Gold status

- Radisson Rewards Gold status

- Accor Plus membership (which comes with a free annual hotel night and bonus ALL status nights)

You can also access a fast-track to Accor Live Limitless with a NAB Rewards credit card. See the Accor website for details.
