## Introduction to Qantas Frequent Flyer

Qantas Frequent Flyer is the loyalty program of Qantas, Australia's flag carrier and largest airline. It is by far the most popular frequent flyer program in Australia - although that doesn't necessarily mean it's the *best value *program available to Australians!

That's not to say it's not worth joining - it absolutely is! There is still much to be gained from this program and, depending on your travel needs, it might work best for you.

Qantas points are the currency of the Qantas Frequent Flyer program. Points can be earned both in the air and on the ground. As we'll discuss shortly, it's extremely easy to earn Qantas points in Australia due to the diverse range of program partners.

These points can then be redeemed for a wide range of rewards. But you'll generally get the most value for your points by spending them on flights or upgrades.

Qantas Loyalty also runs the Qantas Business Rewards Program, and offers Qantas Points Club, Qantas Green Tier and Qantas Club lounge membership. These additional programs and clubs will be covered in future training units.

## Key facts: Qantas Frequent Flyer

Let's begin with a brief overview of the Qantas Frequent Flyer program. Here are some key facts and our program rating:

- **Airline:** Qantas

- **Currency:** Qantas Points

- **Alliance:** Oneworld

- **Status** is earned by accumulating status credits

- **Status levels:** Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Platinum One

- **Qantas points expire** if there is no account activity for more than 18 months

- Points can be transferred between family members' accounts. You may transfer between 5,000 and 600,000 points at a time. There is a transfer limit of 600,000 points per account, per year.

- **Our program rating:** 3.5/5

## Earning Qantas points in the air

Let's now have a look at the different ways to earn Qantas points.

Qantas points can naturally be earned by flying on Qantas or a partner airline. The number of points earned depends on the distance flown, the airline flown and the type of fare you have purchased.

On domestic Qantas flights within Australia, the earn rate depends on the distance flown with flights categorised as short, medium and long flights. All Qantas flights earn a **minimum points guarantee** of 800 Qantas points.

For international flights, the earn rate depends on the geographical zone you're travelling to and from.

All earn rates are published in charts on the Qantas website: Airline Earning Tables

If you find the charts confusing (and you wouldn't be alone), you can also use the Qantas Points Calculators.

You can also earn Qantas points (but generally not status credits) when flying on these other Qantas partner airlines:

- KLM

The Qantas Frequent Flyer program is designed to reward customers the most for flying on Qantas. As such, most Qantas flights earn more points than flights marketed by Oneworld and other partner airlines.

Codeshare flights booked under a "QF" flight number (marketed by Qantas), but operated by another airline, earn at the same higher rates applicable for Qantas flights.

## Earn more points when you fly

Some ways to earn more points (and status credits) when you fly include:

- Travel in a higher class of travel

- Book a more expensive flexible fare

- Book indirect routings

An example of Qantas Economy airfares from Melbourne to Singapore.

In this example, you would earn the following amounts for these Qantas flights from Melbourne to Singapore:

- Sale fare: 2,600 Qantas points + 30 status credits

- Saver fare: 3,900 Qantas points + 40 status credits

- Flex fare: 5,200 Qantas points + 60 status credits

The "Sale" fare earns the fewest points and status credits. It also cannot be upgraded. This is why your fare class is important! We'll explain fare classes in detail in Course 7.

You'll earn points for every individual flight sector flown. The only exception is when booking a flight that has an en-route stopover but continues under the same flight number. An example is QF1 from Sydney to London, which counts as one flight even though there is a refuelling stop along the way.

Why fly direct when you can connect?

This means that you'll earn more points (and status credits) by booking indirect routings! The more flights you take, the more points you'll earn. So it can make sense to book an indirect routing if the price is roughly the same as a direct flight.

You will not earn points for reward flights booked using points. (The only exception is you can earn Qantas status credits if you're a Points Club or Points Club Plus member, and you're flying on a Qantas marketed Classic Flight Reward.) Also note that if you upgrade your ticket, you'll only earn points for the class of travel you originally booked.

You can earn points for flights taken in the past. Just submit a "missing points claim" on the Qantas website.

If you're re-routed or your flight is cancelled, you're still entitled to the points you would have earned if you had flown as originally booked. You'll just need to contact the Qantas Frequent Flyer Service Centre and request an "**original routing credit**".

## Earning Qantas points on the ground

Qantas' diverse range of program partners makes it very easy to earn Qantas points on the ground. In fact, you could already be earning points for things you do every day!

## Credit cards

Credit cards are one of the easiest ways to amass frequent flyer points without setting foot onto a plane.

There is a wide range of Australian credit cards that award Qantas points for every dollar spent on the card. So, you could be earning points every time you pay for something by credit card.

That's not all. Many credit cards offer tens of thousands of frequent flyer points as a sign-up bonus to new customers! You can earn (literally) hundreds of thousands of points every year for very little outlay via credit card sign-up bonuses. We discuss credit card sign-up bonuses in much more detail in the next course.

## Shopping

Qantas has a huge range of retail partners from Woolworths to BP. You can earn Qantas points by shopping with any program partner listed on the Qantas website.

Qantas Frequent Flyer partners with BP. Photo: Matt Graham.

With most retailers, you just need to quote your Qantas Frequent Flyer number (or scan your card) when making a purchase. It works a little differently though with Woolworths and Big W. In this instance, you can earn Everyday Rewards points which can be converted to Qantas points. We'll cover lots of tricks to supercharge your earnings with Everyday Rewards in Course 9.

Hundreds of online retailers also participate in the Qantas Shopping Online Mall including eBay, Apple, Microsoft and Myer. You'll earn points for every dollar spent with these retailers by logging into the Qantas Online Mall before clicking through to the retailer's website. Just make sure cookies are enabled so Qantas can track your purchases and award you the correct points.

## Booking hotels, hire cars and activities

Qantas points can also be earned for:

- Booking hotels through Qantas Hotels

- Booking Airbnb rentals through the Qantas website

- Car rentals with Avis, Budget, Hertz and Thrifty

- Booking activities and airport transfers through Qantas Activities (which is powered by Viator)

- Booking a holiday with TripADeal
